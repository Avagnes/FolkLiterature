THE SOCIAL LIFE OF THINGS AND THE CIRCULATION OF KNOWLEDGE

For many centuries, China has been a major supplier of tea to European markets. From the seventeenth to the nineteenth centuries, Bohea, a special tea produced in the northern mountainous region of Fujian in China, was one of its most successful international products. Of all Chinese teas, export volumes of Bohea were by far the largest. This contribution focuses on the history of Bohea, showing that, more than its allegedly superior taste, its convenient location along existing tea routes, or its supposed medical and "magical" effects, its success and global relevance depended on its affordable price and its material characteristics.

几个世纪以来，中国一直是欧洲市场的主要茶叶供应国。17至19世纪，产自中国福建北部山区的特种茶叶——武夷茶（Bohea），是中国最成功的外销产品之一。在所有中国茶叶中，武夷茶的出口量遥遥领先。本文聚焦武夷茶的历史，论证其成功与全球影响力，并非源于所谓的卓越口感、沿现有茶路的便利区位，或是传说中的药用及“神奇”功效，而主要取决于其亲民的价格与独特的物质属性。

Since Bohea is still regarded as one of the finest teas in China, it is often assumed that it has always been a high-quality tea. Contemporary commercials constantly repeat the legendary story of Catherine of Braganza's passion for this drink, which has greatly contributed to its fame. It is said that when Catherine of Braganza arrived from Portugal to marry Charles II of England in 1662, she brought with her a casket of tea. She drank the red-colored liquid at her wedding ceremony, greatly impressing the British aristocrats, and in the following years she continued drinking black tea to comfort her homesickness. Catherine's fondness for tea made it fashionable in England as an elegant drink, at first within the court, and then with the British public at large. However, historical sources reveal that, in the seventeenth century, Bohea was considered an inferior tea.

由于武夷茶至今仍被视为中国最优质的茶叶之一，人们常误以为它向来都是高品质茶叶。当代广告反复渲染布拉干萨的凯瑟琳对这种饮品的钟爱传说，这极大地提升了它的知名度。据说，1662年凯瑟琳从葡萄牙抵达英国嫁给查理二世时，随身携带了一箱茶叶。她在婚礼上饮用了这种红色饮品，给英国贵族留下了深刻印象，此后多年仍坚持饮用红茶以慰藉乡愁。凯瑟琳对茶的喜爱使茶在英国成为时尚雅致的饮品——起初流行于宫廷，随后风靡全国。然而，史料显示，17世纪时，武夷茶被视为低档茶叶。

From the leaves plucked from the Camellia sinensis, tea passed through the hands of different categories of persons: tea grower, tea maker, tea dealer, Chinese tea laborer, imperial tax official, "hong" merchant, inspector, "supercargo," captain, stevedore, tea buyer, retailer, etc., before reaching consumers' teacups. Accompanying the flow of tea across the Indian and the Atlantic Oceans, knowledge of tea also traveled, crossing the ocean to reach western countries. As Arjun Appadurai points out, long-distance intercultural flows of commodities are composed of interconnected, small-scale, homogenous, low-technology locations through which the commodities flow. While knowledge about such commodities is always potentially uneven in a population, it becomes even more so as distances between locations increase, with peoples' different knowledge (or ignorance) of particular commodities creating tensions that have to be negotiated, but which help determine their intercultural flow.

从采摘自茶树的鲜叶，到最终进入消费者的茶杯，茶叶会经过各类人群的手：茶农、制茶工、茶商、中国茶工、朝廷税官、行商（hong merchant）、检验员、押运员（supercargo）、船长、搬运工、购茶商、零售商等。随着茶叶跨越印度洋与大西洋的流动，相关知识也随之传播至西方国家。正如阿尔君·阿帕杜莱（Arjun Appadurai）所指出的，大宗商品的跨文化长途流动，由相互关联、小规模、同质化的低技术节点构成，商品正是通过这些节点流转。人群中关于这类商品的知识本就可能存在不均，而随着地理距离的增加，这种不均会愈发显著——人们对特定商品的认知差异（或无知）会引发需要调和的张力，但正是这种张力在一定程度上决定了商品的跨文化流动轨迹。

Commodities like Bohea tea represent very complex social forms and distributions of knowledge, which, broadly speaking, can be of two sorts: knowledge (technical, social, aesthetic, and so forth) that goes into the production of the commodity, and knowledge that goes into its "proper" consumption. The two are quite distinct and diverge even more as the spatial, temporal, and social distance between producers and consumers increases. Knowledge, information, and ignorance are thus not just defined in terms of the production and consumption poles of the careers of commodities; they characterize the actual process of circulation and exchange. For this reason, it is particularly interesting to follow the social life of Bohea to see what actually contributed to making it the best-selling tea for almost two centuries. As this contribution will show, despite the many attributes associated with Bohea, it is, first and foremost, its particular materiality that was the key to its success.

武夷茶这类商品承载着极为复杂的社会形态与知识分布，大致可分为两类：一是投入商品生产的知识（技术、社会、审美等层面），二是关乎商品“正确”消费方式的知识。这两类知识差异显著，且随着生产者与消费者在空间、时间和社会层面的距离拉大，差异会进一步扩大。因此，知识、信息与无知并非仅定义于商品生命周期的生产端与消费端，更贯穿于流通与交换的全过程。正因如此，追溯武夷茶的“社会生命”，探究它为何能在近两个世纪里成为最畅销的茶叶，具有特殊的研究价值。正如本文将要揭示的，尽管武夷茶被赋予诸多特质，但归根结底，其独特的物质属性才是成功的核心关键。

This chapter discusses three steps along the routes taken by this tea. It starts by looking at the tea's origin in the Wuyi Mountains in the north of Fujian. It follows its route through the Chinese treaty port cities by focusing on the handling of two major trading companies: the Dutch Vereenigde Oostindische Compagnie (VOC) in the early stage of the Sino-West trade, and the British East India Company (EIC) from the eighteenth to nineteenth centuries. Finally, it discusses consumption of Bohea by foreigners.

本章将围绕武夷茶的流转路径展开，重点分析三个关键环节：首先探究其原产地——福建北部的武夷山；其次追踪其通过中国通商口岸的流转过程，聚焦中西贸易初期的荷兰东印度公司（VOC）与18至19世纪的英国东印度公司（EIC）这两大贸易巨头的运作；最后探讨外国人对武夷茶的消费情况。

BOHEA'S ORIGINS AND THE ACCUMULATION OF COMMERCIAL TEA KNOWLEDGE

The name "Bohea," Wuyi (武夷) in Mandarin, can be very confusing for present-day consumers. Bohea is the name of both the producing region and of its special tea. Furthermore, two different teas are produced in the Wuyi area: Wuyi Rock Tea (oolong tea) and Lapsang Souchong (black tea). Identifying Bohea is thus controversial, and it is not known to which category the name referred to in the early global tea trade. Since 2005, the Lapsang Souchong produced in Wuyi has gone through a cultural and commercial revival in China, where black tea was not part of the tea-drinking tradition. Not surprisingly, modern advertisements for Lapsang Souchong refer to the British craze for black tea in earlier days. Local literati and tea-factory owners all repeat this same story about its origins:

“Bohea”（普通话读作“Wuyi/武夷”）这一名称对当代消费者而言颇为费解——它既是产区名称，也是该产区特种茶叶的名称。此外，武夷山区出产两种不同的茶叶：武夷岩茶（乌龙茶）与正山小种（红茶）。因此，“Bohea”所指的茶叶品类一直存在争议，在早期全球茶叶贸易中，该名称具体指代哪一类茶尚无定论。2005年以来，产自武夷的正山小种在中国经历了文化与商业层面的复兴（中国本土本无饮用红茶的传统）。不足为奇的是，现代正山小种的广告总会提及早年英国人对红茶的狂热追捧。当地文人与茶厂主都在复述着同一个起源传说：

One day, during the turmoil of the late Ming Dynasty, a troop of soldiers passed through the village of Tongmu (桐木) in Wuyi Mountain during the tea-harvesting season. It was getting dark, and soldiers needed to find a place to stay overnight. A tea-processing factory along the road caught their eyes. The factory owners and tea workers had fled to the nearby mountains to hide in advance of the soldiers' arrival, leaving unprocessed tea leaves on the floors of the factory in the rush. The soldiers were very tired and the fresh leaves seemed soft, so they slept on them for one night before leaving early in the morning. After the soldiers' departure, the tea workers returned to the factory and found the tea leaves dehydrated and turning black due to the soldiers' bodily warming. It was no longer possible to dry these leaves according to the regular method. Yet, they did not want to waste them. Therefore, they cut down some locally grown masson pine trees and dried them with a pinewood fire. The tea made in this way looked black and had a smoky flavor. The Chinese market would not like this strange tea, and so the villagers packed the tea for the nearby Xingcun fair and sold it at a very low price. This "special tea" was bought by the Dutch merchants and later sold to the British. To the locals' surprise, the smoked tea captured British hearts and resulted in a pre-order of this tea for the following year at a price two to three times higher than normal. From then on, the locals specialized in producing this black tea for the foreign trade.

明末战乱时期的一天，采茶季的武夷山下桐木村迎来一队士兵。天色渐暗，士兵们需寻找过夜之处，路边一家茶厂引起了他们的注意。茶厂主与工人早已闻讯逃至附近山中躲藏，匆忙间未加工的鲜叶散落满地。士兵们疲惫不堪，见鲜叶柔软，便躺在上面过夜，次日清晨离去。士兵离开后，茶工返回茶厂，发现鲜叶因人体温热而脱水变黑，已无法按常规方法烘干。但他们不愿浪费，于是砍伐当地生长的马尾松，用松火烘干茶叶。这样制成的茶叶色泽乌黑，带有烟熏味。由于中国市场不会接受这种“奇特”的茶叶，村民们便将其打包运往附近的星村集市，以极低的价格出售。这批“特种茶”被荷兰商人购得，后转售给英国人。令当地人意外的是，这种烟熏茶深受英国人喜爱，他们甚至预付了次年的订单，出价是普通茶叶的两三倍。此后，当地人便专门为对外贸易生产这种红茶。

Although this widespread story about the origins of Lapsang Souchong is likely to have been made up just to enhance the historical tie between black tea and the British, it still reflects Chinese people's general appreciation of tea at that time and how they imagined the British craze for black tea. Local people credited the success of Lapsang Souchong on the international market to a series of "accidents." The earliest written record of exports of Bohea appeared in 1640: "Chongzhen 13th year of Ming Dynasty (1368-1644), black teas, including Congou, Bohea, Souchong, and Pokoe, began to be exported to Britain via the Netherlands." In this legendary story, the Chinese and foreign markets had contrasting opinions about the quality of these teas. The value of Lapsang Souchong (Bohea) resulted from a misunderstanding and a gap in communication, but the foreigners' ignorance benefited the business of the local tea makers. After indirect feedback from the middlemen, the local literati imagined and recorded how respectfully the British consumed Bohea: "the English said that the color of Bohea, as red as the agate, and its quality, was far superior to those produced in India and Ceylon. Whenever they were served with Bohea, the guests would stand up to show their appreciation."

尽管这个广为流传的正山小种起源故事很可能是后人杜撰，目的是强化红茶与英国的历史联结，但它仍反映了当时中国人对茶叶的普遍珍视，以及他们对英国人“红茶狂热”的想象。当地人将正山小种的国际成功归因于一系列“偶然”。武夷茶出口的最早文字记载见于1640年：“明崇祯十三年（1368-1644年），工夫茶、武夷茶、小种茶、白毫茶等红茶，经荷兰首次出口英国。”在这个传说中，中外市场对这类茶叶的品质认知存在巨大反差。正山小种（武夷茶）的价值源于一场误解与沟通鸿沟，但外国人的“无知”却为当地茶农带来了商机。通过中间商的间接反馈，当地文人想象并记录了英国人对武夷茶的尊崇：“英人言武夷茶色红如玛瑙，质远胜印度、锡兰所产。每逢宴饮奉此茶，宾客必起而立之，以表敬意。”

The overlapping use of Bohea and Lapsang Souchong, and the use of a dialect word to name it, are significant and also reflect the connection between the tea's origin and the port city (Xiamen) from where it was shipped. In 1684, the Qing government lifted restrictions on maritime trade and Xiamen became one of the four ports open to foreigners. Foreign traders therefore bought the tea in Xiamen and drew from the local Minnan dialect the word "Bohea," really the Wuyi mountainous region, to refer in general to teas from Wuyi.

“Bohea”与“正山小种”的名称混用，以及用方言词汇命名茶叶的现象，具有重要意义——这反映了茶叶原产地与出口港（厦门）的关联。1684年，清政府解除海禁，厦门成为四大通商口岸之一。外国商人在厦门采购茶叶时，借用闽南方言中指代“武夷山区”的“Bohea”一词，泛指产自武夷的各类茶叶。

For the success of any product, two factors are crucial to middlemen and consumers alike: price and quality. Foreign merchants always complained about the poor quality and high price of the tea provided by the hong merchants. To break the imperial blockade and the hong merchants' monopoly of the tea supply, a few employees of western companies, including Samuel Ball and Robert Fortune, who both reached the Bohea hills, visited the tea-producing area, disregarding the Qing government's prohibition on entering the interior of the empire. Multiple reasons explain these risky journeys. First was the need to collect as much knowledge as possible about tea to be able to negotiate better with the hong merchants, instead of depending entirely on middlemen for the deal. Secondly, there was an aim to build direct business ties with local tea suppliers, thus avoiding profit draining to agencies in the port cities. The third and most challenging reason was to transplant tea plants to British colonial possessions. This last mission, however, could be achieved only with imperial-level support.

对中间商与消费者而言，任何产品的成功都离不开两个关键因素：价格与品质。外国商人常抱怨行商提供的茶叶质次价高。为打破清政府的封锁与行商的茶叶供应垄断，包括塞缪尔·鲍尔（Samuel Ball）与罗伯特·福钧（Robert Fortune）在内的几位西方公司雇员，不顾清政府禁止外国人进入内地的规定，深入武夷茶区考察。他们的冒险之旅背后有多重原因：一是为收集尽可能多的茶叶知识，以便在与行商谈判时占据主动，而非完全依赖中间商；二是希望与当地茶农建立直接贸易关系，避免利润被口岸代理商侵蚀；三是最具挑战性的目标——将茶树种移植到英国殖民地。不过，最后一项任务唯有获得帝国层面的支持才能实现。

Robert Fortune, a Scottish plant hunter hired by the EIC, undertook a three-year plant-collecting expedition to southern China and eventually transplanted 20,000 tea plants and seedlings to the Darjeeling region of India. The Bohea hills were Fortune's major destination. One of his most significant discoveries for the Western world was that black tea and green tea originated from the same variety of tea plant and simply resulted from different processing methods. Before Fortune's visit to the Bohea hills, some British authors thought that the black teas were produced from a plant called by botanists Thea bohea, the green one from Thea viridis. Fortune had already been in the Singlo Hills, an area renowned for its green tea plants, where the Hyson, Gunpowder, Singlo, and Twankay green teas were produced. Since the Bohea region was known to produce black tea, he assumed that the plants were different. Yet, to his great surprise, he found out that the plants in the Bohea region were exactly the same as those in the green tea districts of the north. Thus, Bohea refers to tea from this region, regardless of whether it is green or black.

英国东印度公司雇佣的苏格兰植物猎人罗伯特·福钧，在中国南方开展了为期三年的植物采集远征，最终将2万株茶树与茶苗移植到印度的大吉岭地区。武夷山区是福钧的主要目的地。他为西方世界带来的最重大发现之一是：红茶与绿茶源自同一品种的茶树，仅加工方法不同。在福钧到访武夷之前，一些英国学者认为，红茶产自植物学家所称的“武夷茶种（Thea bohea）”，而绿茶产自“绿茶种（Thea viridis）”。福钧曾到访以种植绿茶闻名的松萝山（Singlo Hills），那里出产熙春茶、珠茶、松萝茶与屯溪绿茶。由于武夷地区以产红茶著称，他起初推测两地茶树品种不同。但令他震惊的是，武夷山区的茶树与北方绿茶产区的茶树完全一致。因此，“Bohea”实际指产自该地区的茶叶，无论其为绿茶还是红茶。

In 1836, earlier than Fortune's expedition, a French traveler had already visited the Bohea hills and figured out that green tea and black tea originated from the same plant, being distinguished only by the length of time the leaves were dried. He also mentioned that the method of frying tea was a sort of national secret. With the curiosity of the foreign visitor, he recorded the method of processing black tea, including the last step, the addition of calcium sulfate and indigo powder to obtain its dark color (indigo enhanced the color, while calcium sulfate fixed it). Although this French adventurer recorded it as a regular operation, fraud apparently affected Bohea from the beginning, to be followed by many other instances throughout this commodity's entire production and marketing chain.

早在福钧远征之前的1836年，一位法国旅行者就已到访武夷山区，并发现绿茶与红茶源自同一茶树，仅通过茶叶烘干时间的长短加以区分。他还提到，炒茶工艺在中国堪称“国家机密”。出于外国访客的好奇心，他记录了红茶的加工方法，包括最后一步：添加硫酸钙与靛蓝粉末以获得深色泽（靛蓝增强色泽，硫酸钙固定色泽）。尽管这位法国探险家将其记录为常规操作，但显然，欺诈行为从武夷茶贸易之初就已存在，并贯穿于该商品的整个生产与营销链条。

TREATY CITIES: THE ADULTERATION OF BOHEA AND WESTERN MERCHANTS' STRATEGIES AND RESPONSES

Purchasing luxurious and exotic goods such as tea, silk, and porcelain was quite profitable, yet it was also a risky commercial activity that relied on specific expertise. On the one hand, building upon their repeated negotiations with the hong merchants and accumulated experience, foreign tea buyers gradually developed a capacity to distinguish different categories of teas and check the quality of purchased tea. On the other hand, as tea was completely unknown to western consumers, selling it in the domestic market was not just limited to selling products to retailers and housewives directly, it also involved a series of knowledge transfers on how to prepare and consume tea. As middlemen, the so-called supercargoes were not only in charge of purchasing teas from China, they also played the role of disseminating knowledge.

采购茶叶、丝绸、瓷器等奢华异国商品利润丰厚，但也是一项依赖专业知识的高风险商业活动。一方面，通过与行商的反复谈判和经验积累，外国购茶商逐渐具备了区分茶叶品类、检验茶叶品质的能力；另一方面，由于西方消费者对茶叶完全陌生，在国内市场销售茶叶不仅是直接向零售商与家庭主妇售卖产品，还涉及一系列关于茶叶冲泡与饮用方法的知识传递。作为中间商，所谓的“押运员（supercargo）”不仅负责从中国采购茶叶，还承担着知识传播的角色。

All tea buyers had to learn the various processes of producing tea from the Chinese brokers in order to develop their own ability to assess the quality of the teas, and to carefully judge and select which knowledge had to be confined in China and which information should not be circulated beyond the cargo ship (for instance, certain blending and packaging practices involving regular adulteration had to remain confidential within the tea-importing company). They were not only learning passively but were also engaged in producing an appreciation and knowledge of tea that were beneficial to tea companies and which had to be passed on to their consumers at home.

所有购茶商都必须向中国经纪人学习茶叶的各类生产流程，以培养自身的茶叶品质评估能力，同时谨慎判断并筛选哪些知识需限制在中国境内、哪些信息不得传播至货船之外（例如，涉及常规掺假的拼配与包装工艺，必须在茶叶进口公司内部保密）。他们并非被动学习，还主动构建有利于茶企的茶叶品鉴知识体系，并将其传递给本国消费者。

The Chinese hong merchants and the western supercargoes were two key roles representing the seller and the buyer during the period of the Canton system. The supercargoes, as the company's representatives on each voyage, enforced the terms of these agreements and were held accountable by the company's directors for any variation in the detail of the shipped goods.

行商与西方押运员是广州制度时期代表买卖双方的核心角色。押运员作为公司每次航行的代表，负责执行协议条款，若所运货物细节与约定存在差异，需向公司董事承担责任。

The Canton system was disadvantageous to the foreign merchants, but it benefited the Chinese hong merchants, who had direct access to information. Foreign tea buyers in the treaty cities had little knowledge of either the Chinese market for their imports or the Chinese producers of their exports, and had only a vague picture of the tea-growing areas. Most hongs simply operated as brokers or, more commonly, as contractors who took delivery of tea for resale and export. Hong merchants basically profited from the information they had, their contacts with the tea suppliers, and the privilege of engaging in foreign trade. By contrast, the supercargo, who was in charge of business with the Chinese hong merchants, had to show diplomatic skills in dealing with all these extraordinary situations, having not simply the courage to resist extortionate demands, but also the ability to conduct trade in the face of the demands made. The supercargoes had to be really knowledgeable and very careful about tea quality in order to evaluate the various sorts of tea and decide which one would prove more profitable and be worth buying.

广州制度对外国商人不利，却让掌握信息优势的行商受益。通商口岸的外国购茶商既不了解中国的进口商品市场，也不熟悉出口商品的中国生产者，对茶叶产区的认知更是模糊。大多数行商仅扮演经纪人角色，更常见的是作为承包商——接收茶叶后转售出口。行商的利润主要来源于信息优势、与茶农的联系以及从事对外贸易的特权。相比之下，负责与行商对接业务的押运员，必须具备外交手腕以应对各类特殊情况：既要敢于抵制勒索要求，又要能在复杂诉求下开展贸易。押运员必须具备深厚的专业知识，对茶叶品质保持高度谨慎，才能评估各类茶叶的盈利潜力，判断哪些值得采购。

From 1757 to 1842, due to the Qing government's policy of banning the maritime trade, Guangzhou was the only port to remain open and deal with foreign trade. After the Sino-British Treaty of Nanking (Nanjing) in 1842, Xiamen, Fuzhou, Ningbo, and Shanghai were also opened up as treaty ports. Every spring, numerous boats full of tea leaves traveled from the Bohea region through the internal river network to reach these five port cities. A local intellectual in Chong'an (an old name for Wuyi) depicted the flourishing scene as follows:

1757年至1842年，受清政府海禁政策影响，广州成为唯一对外开放的通商口岸。1842年《中英南京条约》签订后，厦门、福州、宁波、上海也相继成为通商口岸。每年春季，无数载满茶叶的船只从武夷山区出发，通过内河航运网络运往这五个港口城市。崇安（武夷旧称）一位当地文人这样描绘当时的繁荣景象：

Around Grain Rain season, to Nantai (Fuzhou) tea was shipped, Simultaneously, Amoy (Xiamen), Guangzhou, and Chaoshan were opened, Going to Bohea is in pursuit of nothing, But with fresh tea leaves boats are filled.

“谷雨前后，茶运南台（福州）；厦、广、潮汕，同时开港。赴武夷者，无所求焉，唯满船鲜叶而归。”

As demand for Bohea in western countries increased, some foreign companies were no longer satisfied with merely purchasing tea from Chinese middlemen and began sending commercial spies to tea-producing areas. Robert Fortune's mission for the EIC was unquestionably a successful example. In addition to shipping tea seedlings to British India, he also brought skilled workers on contract to India. He was keenly aware that, to transplant tea plants overseas, knowledge about cultivating and processing the tea should not be separated from the tea plants themselves. Thus, his job was not merely collecting plants, it also involved hiring and shipping the people who carried the embodied knowledge on a long-distance voyage.

随着西方国家对武夷茶的需求增长，部分外国公司不再满足于通过中国中间商采购茶叶，开始派遣商业间谍潜入茶叶产区。罗伯特·福钧为英国东印度公司执行的任务无疑是成功的典范：他不仅将茶苗运往英属印度，还雇佣了熟练茶工赴印。他敏锐地意识到，茶树种的海外移植离不开配套的种植与加工知识——因此，他的工作不仅是收集植物，还包括雇佣并运送掌握这些“嵌入式知识”的工人进行长途航行。

When Fuzhou became an important tea trading port in 1853, some foreign companies like Russell & Co. began sending their Chinese staff to Wuyi Mountain to make purchases directly from the growers. This "up-country" system soon became the standard procedure for large companies. Tea purchased up-country was available for shipment several weeks ahead of "market tea" and at a lower cost. The crucial reason for western companies' preference for Bohea was certainly its low price. Data from 1780 confirm that the price of Bohea was the lowest of all the tea products that the VOC purchased from China that year. Accounting for as much as 64.27 percent of total trading volume in 1780, the prices of Bohea were only 0.36-0.5 guilders per pound, while the price of Gunpowder tea, by contrast, with a trading volume of only 0.12 percent, reached as high as 2.16-2.23 guilders per pound.

1853年福州成为重要茶叶贸易港口后，罗素公司（Russell & Co.）等外国企业开始派遣中国雇员前往武夷山，直接向茶农采购茶叶。这种“深入产区”的采购模式很快成为大公司的标准流程——产区直购的茶叶比“市场茶”早数周即可装船，且成本更低。西方公司青睐武夷茶的核心原因无疑是其低廉的价格。1780年的数据显示，武夷茶是荷兰东印度公司当年从中国采购的所有茶叶中价格最低的品类：其交易量占总贸易量的64.27%，每磅价格仅为0.36-0.5荷兰盾；而珠茶的交易量仅占0.12%，每磅价格却高达2.16-2.23荷兰盾。

According to VOC purchase lists from 1757 to 1783, compared to the other eight tea products imported from China, only the home order for Bohea was unrestricted and simply described as "as much as possible," while other teas all had a recommended amount each year. The EIC contracts for 1793 also show that, compared to the prices of 27 teals per picul for Congo, 25 teals per picul for Twankay, and 57 teals per picul for Hyson, Bohea cost only 13.5 teals per picul. These data from the two major western import companies demonstrate that its profitability, rather than its quality, was what attracted western tea buyers. This economic rationality is also confirmed by the low rank attributed to Bohea by George Sigmond in his famous book on teas. Given its low price, it was not necessary to adulterate it with inferior tea (a rather common practice for the finest green teas), Its market cost reflected its lower status compared to other teas, disproving the local tea growers present-day claim that Bohea has always been a superior quality product.

从1757年至1783年荷兰东印度公司的采购清单来看，与其他八种进口中国茶叶相比，只有武夷茶的国内订单无数量限制，仅标注“尽可能多”，而其他茶叶每年都有推荐采购量。1793年英国东印度公司的合同也显示，工夫茶每担价格27两、屯溪绿茶25两、熙春茶57两，而武夷茶每担仅13.5两。这两大西方进口巨头的数据表明，吸引外国购茶商的是武夷茶的盈利空间，而非品质。乔治·西格蒙德（George Sigmond）在其著名的茶叶专著中将武夷茶列为低等级，也印证了这一经济逻辑。由于价格低廉，武夷茶无需像高档绿茶那样掺假（掺假是高档绿茶的常见操作），其市场价格反映了它低于其他茶叶的地位，这也反驳了当代当地茶农声称武夷茶“向来品质卓越”的说法。

The tea business traditionally involved five steps: negotiating a contract, signing the contract, examining the tea samples, packing the teas, and loading and stowing the teas on the company's ships. The contract steps were based on mutual trust between the supercargoes and the hong merchants and their long-term cooperation (although back-and-forth negotiations could last for months), and the trading conditions were clearly described in the contracts. Thus, fraud normally occurred when the tea was packed and loaded. Also, the loading order of the vessels indicates that Bohea was shipped as a cheap tea, and sometimes even served as ballast. Morse notes that the EIC put a quantity of Bohea on board every ship to serve as ballast, though later it was loaded to secure adequate flooring for the finer teas. The VOC vessels practiced a similar loading order: the more valuable porcelain was loaded at the bottom, followed by Bohea, the cheapest tea, used to fill the porcelain chests in order to protect them.

传统茶叶贸易包含五个环节：谈判、签约、验样、包装、装船仓储。签约环节基于押运员与行商的互信及长期合作（尽管谈判可能持续数月），合同中明确规定贸易条款。因此，欺诈行为通常发生在包装与装船阶段。此外，船舶的装载顺序也表明武夷茶是廉价茶叶，有时甚至被用作压舱物。霍齐亚·巴卢·莫尔斯（Hosea Ballou Morse）指出，英国东印度公司的每艘船都会装载一定量的武夷茶作为压舱物，后来则用于为高档茶叶铺垫底层。荷兰东印度公司的船舶也采用类似装载顺序：最贵重的瓷器装在最底层，上方装载最便宜的武夷茶，填充瓷箱间隙以起到保护作用。

Most of the time the tea leaves had already been crushed when loading at the port because of the stevedores' lack of concern and could never meet the quality described in the contract. A daily record of the loading process made by a supercargo on November 3, 1764, shows that the task of packing tea was delegated to the Chinese forced contract laborers (known as "coolies") employed by the tea-supplying agents. In packing it, the laborers rammed the tea into the chests by trampling on it with their feet. According to the daily record for November 3, 1764: "Each nation which is packing screams a thousand times a day: do not grind the tea to dust, but stamp it straight up and down!' and perhaps one has 100 chests which are already half full thrown back upon the heap of tea which is not yet packed, because the tea has been ground to dust." Since the middle of the eighteenth century, Dutch home tea buyers frequently complained to the VOC about the dusty nature of the tea, Bohea in particular.

由于搬运工操作粗糙，茶叶在港口装船时往往已被压碎，根本无法达到合同约定的品质。1764年11月3日，一位押运员的装船日记记载，茶叶包装工作由茶商雇佣的中国契约劳工（“苦力”）负责，劳工们用脚将茶叶踩入木箱压实。日记中写道：“各国负责包装的人员每天嘶吼上千次：‘别把茶叶碾成粉末，垂直踩踏！’但仍有近百箱已装半满的茶叶被倒回原料堆——因为茶叶已被碾成粉尘。”18世纪中叶以来，荷兰国内的购茶商频繁向荷兰东印度公司抱怨茶叶（尤其是武夷茶）含尘量过高。

Western tea buyers were constantly confronted with adulteration by Chinese merchants. Sigmond points out that there were many counterfeit Boheas: two kinds of Bohea were shipped from China, Bohea from Fujian, and a lower quality tea manufactured on the spot and therefore called "Canton Bohea," a mixture of refuse Congou with a coarse tea called Woping. An 1830 source relates that one-third of the company's Bohea tea was in fact produced in Woping, northern Guangdong. This kind of commercial deceit was more frequent in the early stages, when western supercargoes were still ignorant about Chinese tea. Western tea buyers, however, gradually developed the ability to judge the quality of tea and began returning considerable quantities of teas on the grounds that they had been wrongly packed or artificially colored, or were inferior to the declared chop, and demanding that the Chinese merchants replace them. In 1783, no fewer than 1,402 chests, many containing Bohea, were returned in this way out of the shipments from 1781.

外国购茶商还频繁遭遇中国商人的掺假行为。西格蒙德指出，武夷茶存在大量仿冒品：从中国出口的“武夷茶”分为两类——福建产武夷茶，以及在广州本地制造的低档茶“广州武夷茶”（Canton Bohea），后者由工夫茶废料与广东北部的粗茶“沃平茶（Woping）”混合而成。1830年的一份资料显示，公司采购的武夷茶中，有三分之一实为沃平茶。这种商业欺诈在早期更为普遍，当时西方押运员对中国茶叶仍缺乏了解。但随着外国购茶商逐渐掌握茶叶品质判断能力，他们开始以“包装不当、人工染色、品质不符印章标注”等为由，大量退回茶叶并要求中国商人更换。1783年，仅1781年批次的货物中，就有不少于1402箱茶叶（其中多为武夷茶）以这种方式被退回。

THE HOME MARKET: BRANDING BOHEA AS A SUCCESSFUL TEA PRODUCT

Although the imported price of Bohea was the lowest amongst the teas in China, consumers abroad knew nothing about tea. Their knowledge about making, appreciating, and selecting tea was highly dependent on the advertisements of tea companies and retailers' explanations. These agencies not only sold the product, they also screened, edited, and selectively shared their knowledge with their customers. They were supposed to be knowledgeable enough to direct home consumers in how to choose and make tea properly.

尽管武夷茶是中国出口茶叶中价格最低的，但国外消费者对茶叶一无所知，其冲泡、品鉴与选购知识高度依赖茶企广告与零售商的讲解。这些机构不仅售卖产品，还对茶叶知识进行筛选、编辑与选择性传递，需具备足够专业知识，指导本国消费者正确选茶、泡茶。

During the eighteenth century, Bohea was one of three kinds of tea generally available on the British market alongside two forms of green tea, Singlo and Imperial. Compared to them, Bohea had two obvious advantages: it was more tolerant of long sea journeys (the flavor of green tea could disappear after a year-long sea voyage), and it was regarded as more resilient when being processed. While green tea can only be served as an infusion, Bohea's robust leaves tolerate reheating or even repeated boiling. These advantages explain its commercial success, as it was much more affordable for ordinary consumers, including in the emerging working classes.

18世纪的英国市场上，武夷茶与松萝茶（Singlo）、特级绿茶（Imperial）是三种常见茶叶。相较于后两者，武夷茶有两大显著优势：一是耐长途海运（绿茶的风味在一年的海上航行后可能流失），二是加工适应性更强——绿茶仅能冲泡饮用，而武夷茶的粗韧叶片可反复加热甚至煮沸。这些优势奠定了其商业成功的基础，使其成为包括新兴工人阶级在内的普通消费者都能负担得起的饮品。

The increasing supply of sugar, resulting from British colonial expansion in the Caribbean islands in the eighteenth century, also greatly stimulated the consumption of black tea. For the British public, "a nice cup of tea" needed two elements: tea and sugar, both products reliant on the development of overseas plantations and global trade. As Mintz insightfully points out, the British addiction to sugar and tea was not the result of a "natural" taste preference, but was driven instead by the huge wealth that global tea and sugar trade could bring to the Empire.

18世纪英国在加勒比海地区的殖民扩张带来了蔗糖供应的增长，这也极大地刺激了红茶消费。对英国民众而言，“一杯好茶”需要两个要素：茶叶与糖——这两种产品的普及都依赖海外种植园的发展与全球贸易。正如西德尼·明茨（Sidney W. Mintz）深刻指出的，英国人对糖与茶的痴迷并非源于“天然”的口味偏好，而是由全球茶糖贸易可为帝国带来的巨额财富所驱动。

The term "Bohea" first appeared in the Western world in 1696, in A Voyage of Surat in the Year 1689 by John Ovington, where three kinds of tea are described: Bing, Singlo, and Bohea. The first two were green teas; only Bohea was a "red" tea. In 1755, Samuel Johnson defined Bohea in his Dictionary of the English Language as "a species of tea, of higher color, and more astringent taste, than green tea." In 1838, Charles Alexander Bruce's An Account of the Manufacture of Black Tea, though not mentioning the word "Bohea," provides the first description of the method of making black teas, proving that this tea product was the most highly appreciated by the British at that time.

“Bohea”一词于1696年首次出现在西方文献中——约翰·奥文顿（John Ovington）在《1689年苏拉特航行记》（A Voyage of Surat in the Year 1689）中描述了三种茶叶：饼茶（Bing）、松萝茶（Singlo）与武夷茶（Bohea），前两者为绿茶，唯有武夷茶是“红色”茶叶。1755年，塞缪尔·约翰逊（Samuel Johnson）在《英语词典》中将武夷茶定义为“一种色泽更深、口感更涩的茶叶，区别于绿茶”。1838年，查尔斯·亚历山大·布鲁斯（Charles Alexander Bruce）的《红茶制造工艺述评》（An Account of the Manufacture of Black Tea）虽未提及“Bohea”一词，却首次详细描述了红茶的制作方法，印证了这类茶叶在当时深受英国人推崇。

By the middle of the nineteenth century, teas from China had become more affordable than ever before, which resulted in tea consumption spreading from the upper class to millions of households. To meet the increasing demand for Chinese tea among ordinary British who still lacked a basic knowledge of teas, some mass publications and guidebooks were put on the market. Sigmond's Tea: Its Effects, Medicinal and Moral provided practical knowledge for differentiating teas from one another in the form of detailed descriptions of major tea commodities from China, which became a sort of guide for British consumers in the nineteenth century. While admitting that the Chinese are tea connoisseurs, he points out that Bohea is not widely consumed in its country of origin (China), where even the humbler classes do not purchase it. This clarification is consistent with the story of the origins of Bohea/lapsang Souchong, which stresses that this tea was disliked by the Chinese but favored by the British.

由于武夷茶是当时消费量最大的茶叶，西格蒙德在书中对其着墨最多。他承认中国人是品茶行家，但同时指出，武夷茶在其原产地中国并未广泛饮用，甚至底层民众也不会购买。这一说明与武夷茶/正山小种的起源传说一致——该传说强调这种茶叶不受中国人喜爱，却深受英国人青睐。

When Bohea appeared on the London market, its scarcity and its flavorful taste made it popular with consumers and allowed it to sell at a high price. Within a few years, London tea brokers took advantage of the relative inexperience of the smaller retailers and their customers and began selling low-quality black tea from other regions, especially the counterfeit Woping, under the name of Bohea, so causing the progressive decline of its reputation. Gradually, in the eighteenth century, oxidized Bohea became synonymous with black tea in general and even with Chinese tea, as the method of fermentation used to produce black tea progressively stabilized and spread throughout China.

武夷茶初登伦敦市场时，因稀缺性与独特风味深受消费者喜爱，价格居高不下。但短短几年内，伦敦茶叶经纪人利用小型零售商及其客户的经验不足，开始将其他地区的低档红茶（尤其是仿冒的沃平茶）以“武夷茶”之名售卖，导致其声誉逐渐下滑。18世纪以来，随着红茶发酵工艺逐渐成熟并在中国各地推广，“氧化处理的武夷茶”逐渐成为红茶的代名词，甚至被用来泛指中国茶叶。

This decline in its reputation also resulted from the increasing knowledge of tea among home consumers and the development of more subtle marketing strategies by the tea companies. While in the 1690s tea merchants described their best products in advertisements as "extraordinary superfine tea" without giving any specific tea name, by the first decade of the eighteenth century, East India Company sales began to specify different parcels of "Fine Singlo Tea" and "Bohea Tea" and to note the difference between "Green Tea" and "Bohea Tea." By 1712, sales records divided tea into five categories: Bohea, Pekoe, Bing, Congou, and Singlo. These distinctions between teas of different manufacture and origin, along with the increasingly complex variety of flavors, aroma, and appearance, reflected the growing sophistication of the tea market and a greater curiosity about kinds of tea and modes of description and appreciation.

武夷茶声誉的下滑，还源于本国消费者茶叶知识的增长与茶企更精细的营销策略。17世纪90年代，茶叶广告中最优质的产品仅被描述为“特级好茶”，未标注具体名称；而到18世纪第一个十年，英国东印度公司的销售记录开始明确区分“优质松萝茶”“武夷茶”，并标注“绿茶”与“武夷茶”的差异。1712年，销售记录已将茶叶分为五类：武夷茶、白毫茶、饼茶、工夫茶、松萝茶。这种基于制作工艺与产地的分类，以及风味、香气、外形的多样化，反映了茶叶市场的日益成熟，也体现了人们对茶叶品类、描述方式与品鉴方法的好奇心不断增强。

Commercial secrets regarding the packing and branding of tea were inseparable from a knowledge of tea selection, grading, and screening. This doomed the tea companies and the retailers. Even though they pretended to guide local consumers by distributing instruction manuals and posting commercials, this "knowledge sharing" was necessarily reserved: they were only willing to share knowledge that was beneficial to their business and not the grading and branding secrets, as they always had to be more knowledgeable and smarter than their customers. Along the commercial chain, from the source in the Bohea hills in China to the end of the chain in western consumers' teacups, adulteration and fraud happened at all stages. In their own interests, Chinese producers, hong merchants, and international EIC and VOC tea buyers either mixed tea with other substances or deceived the downstream middlemen in other ways. Dutch home tea buyers complained that the company's coarsely packed "VOC teas" were heavily mixed with dust at the auctions, but in the meantime these same tea buyers benefited from selling the adulterated tea to the retailers, who might adulterate it even further before selling it on to the consumers.

茶叶包装与品牌推广的商业机密，与茶叶的筛选、分级、检测知识密不可分——这让茶企与零售商陷入两难。尽管他们通过发放说明书、张贴广告等方式假装指导消费者，但这种“知识分享”必然有所保留：他们只愿分享对业务有利的信息，绝不会泄露分级与品牌机密，因为他们必须始终比消费者更懂行、更精明。从中国武夷山区的产地到西方消费者的茶杯，整个商业链条的各个环节都存在掺假与欺诈行为：中国生产者、行商、英国东印度公司与荷兰东印度公司的国际购茶商，为了自身利益，或在茶叶中掺假，或以其他方式欺骗下游中间商。荷兰国内的购茶商抱怨公司拍卖的“荷兰东印度公司茶叶”包装粗糙、含尘量高，但与此同时，他们又将掺假茶叶转售给零售商，而零售商在卖给消费者之前可能会进一步掺假。

CONCLUSION

By tracing the circulation of Bohea from its China origins to its major markets abroad, this chapter has demonstrated that its commercial success as a popular international commodity was, in a sense, predestined, and related to its unique materiality. The success of Bohea did not derive from it having a better taste than other teas (as claimed by modern Chinese tea growers), but from its profitable materiality: its leaves were more robust, larger, and more capable of withstanding repeated brewing and even boiling, making it much more affordable than any other tea for the working classes, who became the majority of tea consumers in Britain.

通过追溯武夷茶从中国原产地到海外主要市场的流转轨迹，本章论证了其作为热门国际商品的商业成功在某种意义上是“命中注定”的，且与其独特的物质属性密切相关。武夷茶的成功并非源于比其他茶叶更优的口感（如当代中国茶农所声称的），而是源于其具有盈利潜力的物质属性：叶片粗韧、体积较大，耐反复冲泡甚至煮沸，这使得它比其他任何茶叶都更实惠，成为英国占人口多数的工人阶级的首选饮品。

As a truly transcultural, cross-linguistic and transregional commodity, Bohea was far more than a beverage from China. It was a product connected to chinoiserie, aristocratic taste, and to the material embodiment of an oriental lifestyle. Bohea's commodity value was socially constructed through a complicated entanglement and relationship of dependence between the various agents and the tea itself.

作为一种真正跨文化、跨语言、跨地域的商品，武夷茶远不止是一种来自中国的饮品——它与中国风、贵族品味及东方生活方式的物质载体紧密相连。武夷茶的商品价值是通过各类参与者与茶叶本身之间复杂的交织与依赖关系，被社会建构而成的。

It is very hard to specify what is "authentic" about Bohea, as its identity and significance have been variously constructed and changed over time and space. The authenticity of Bohea derived from the mutual relationality and dependence of human beings and things, rather than from "the order of things" given by humans. It did not stem from apparent, fixed, and essential dualisms--such as truth and falsehood, real and counterfeit, agency and structure, knowledge and power, context and content, or activity and passivity--but rather from a subtle dependency in-between such dualities. As Appadurai emphasizes, the knowledge that is required to produce luxury commodities, where more subjective and contestable criteria of taste, judgment, and individual experience are likely to create sharp variations, cannot be standardized.

很难明确界定武夷茶的“真实性”究竟是什么——其身份与意义随时间和空间的变化而被不同地建构。武夷茶的真实性源于人与物之间的相互关联与依赖，而非人类赋予的“事物秩序”；它并非来自那些显性、固定、本质化的二元对立（如真与假、正品与仿冒品、能动者与结构、知识与权力、语境与内容、主动与被动），而是源于这些二元对立之间微妙的依存关系。正如阿帕杜莱所强调的，奢侈品生产所需的知识涉及主观且有争议的品味、判断与个人体验标准，这些标准可能存在巨大差异，因此无法被标准化。

Local tea growers, tea dealers, early explorers and plant hunters, hong merchants, supercargoes, stevedores, international import companies (VOC and EIC), home tea buyers, retailers, and end consumers--and the list could even be longer--all had the potential to add and contribute to building this knowledge. The higher the number of agents involved in the Bohea chain, the more flexible the interpretation and the opportunities for profit. Middlemen at different levels therefore played important roles in constructing the value of this distant, exotic commodity by collecting, selecting, editing, and transmitting the knowledge necessary to appreciate it.

当地茶农、茶商、早期探险家与植物猎人、行商、押运员、搬运工、国际进口公司（荷兰东印度公司、英国东印度公司）、国内购茶商、零售商、终端消费者——这份名单还可以更长——所有这些参与者都有可能为这种知识的建构添砖加瓦。参与武夷茶链条的参与者越多，对其价值的解读空间就越灵活，盈利机会也就越多。因此，各级中间商通过收集、筛选、编辑与传递品鉴所需的知识，在建构这种遥远异国商品的价值方面发挥了关键作用。

Due to the geographical and linguistic separation of producers and consumers, screening and transmitting knowledge about Bohea were essential. The information that eventually appeared on commercial posters in Western countries was carefully designed, edited, and presented. The popularity of Bohea was certainly not an accident, as is commonly claimed in China, but the result of a well-conceived promotional strategy that highlighted its superiority as an affordable and resilient brew for consumers.

由于生产者与消费者在地理和语言上的隔绝，筛选与传递武夷茶的相关知识变得至关重要。最终出现在西方商业海报上的信息，都是经过精心设计、编辑与呈现的。武夷茶的流行绝非中国坊间所声称的“偶然”，而是源于一套周密的推广策略——该策略凸显了其作为“消费者负担得起、耐冲泡的优质茶饮”的优势。

The successful branding of Bohea perfectly embodies the exquisite marketing skill that facilitated a balance between the Chinese suppliers' concerns for quality and quantity, the emerging working-class consumers' yearning for a bourgeois lifestyle, the purchasing capacity of ordinary families, and the "vanity" of housewives on the one hand, versus the accurate pricing of tea on the other. Throughout the flow of Bohea, taste seems to have been the least important factor.

武夷茶的成功品牌化，完美体现了高超的营销技巧——它巧妙平衡了多重诉求：中国供应商对品质与产量的关注、新兴工人阶级对资产阶级生活方式的向往、普通家庭的购买力、家庭主妇的“虚荣心”，以及茶叶的合理定价。在武夷茶的整个流转过程中，口感似乎是最不重要的因素。



## 总结
本文聚焦17至19世纪中国武夷茶（Bohea）的跨洋贸易史，核心围绕其“物质属性”与“知识流通”展开，系统梳理了武夷茶从产地到海外市场的流转脉络及成功逻辑，具体总结如下：

一、武夷茶的核心定位与成功关键

• 武夷茶产自福建北部武夷山，是该时期中国出口量最大的茶叶品种，但其成功并非源于传说中的“卓越口感”或“贵族背书”（如布拉干萨的凯瑟琳的饮用故事），而是核心依赖亲民价格与独特物质属性——叶片粗韧、耐长途海运、可反复冲泡甚至煮沸，适配普通消费者（尤其是英国工人阶级）的使用场景与购买力。

• 历史上武夷茶的定义存在争议，早期既涵盖武夷岩茶（乌龙茶），也包括正山小种（红茶），后在国际市场中逐渐被“正山小种（Lapsang Souchong）”指代，但“Bohea”一词因贸易传承成为武夷产区茶叶的泛称。

二、产地起源与商业知识的积累

• 武夷茶的外销起源带有“偶然色彩”：明末战乱中，茶农因士兵宿于鲜叶上导致茶叶变质，误打误撞用松火烘干制成烟熏味红茶，被荷兰商人低价收购后转售英国，意外获得追捧，成为外销起点。

• 西方商人为打破中国行商的垄断，冒险深入武夷茶区获取知识：苏格兰植物猎人罗伯特·福钧（受英国东印度公司委托）不仅移植2万株茶苗到印度大吉岭，还带回熟练茶工与种植加工技术，关键发现“红茶与绿茶源于同一茶树，仅加工方式不同”，颠覆了西方此前的认知。

• 早期茶贸易中存在信息差：中国本土不认可武夷茶的“烟熏味”，而西方消费者因缺乏茶叶知识，将其视为特色；西方商人通过实地考察、雇佣本地劳动力等方式，逐步填补产销两端的知识鸿沟。

三、通商口岸的贸易运作与博弈

• 贸易流程：武夷茶通过内河航运从武夷山运往广州、厦门、福州等通商口岸，经行商（hong merchant）与西方押运员（supercargo）对接，完成签约、验样、包装、装船等环节，最终运往欧洲。

• 贸易格局演变：1757-1842年广州独口通商时期，行商凭借信息优势垄断供应；1842年后多口岸开放，西方公司（如罗素公司）直接深入产区采购，降低成本并抢占先机。

• 贸易中的问题与应对：中国行商存在掺假、包装粗糙（茶叶被踩碎、含尘量高）等问题，西方商人通过积累品质鉴别能力、退回不合格货物等方式维权；同时，武夷茶因价格低廉，常被用作船舶压舱物或填充瓷箱的保护物，反映其“低成本大宗商品”的定位。

四、海外市场的消费与品牌建构

• 消费普及：18世纪后，英国殖民扩张带来的蔗糖供应增长，与武夷茶形成“茶+糖”的消费组合，推动饮茶习惯从贵族普及到普通家庭，武夷茶因高性价比成为市场主流。

• 知识传播与品牌塑造：西方茶企与零售商通过广告、指南手册（如西格蒙德《茶：其药用与道德影响》），向消费者传递武夷茶的冲泡、品鉴知识，同时选择性建构其“东方特色”形象，弱化其“低档”属性，平衡消费者对“资产阶级生活方式”的向往与实际购买力。

• 声誉变迁：早期因稀缺性价格高昂，后因仿冒品（如广东沃平茶冒充“广州武夷茶”）泛滥导致声誉下滑，但始终凭借稳定的性价比占据市场主流，成为“中国茶叶”的代名词之一。

五、核心结论

• 武夷茶的成功是跨文化贸易中“物质属性+知识建构”共同作用的结果：其价值并非固定不变，而是由产地茶农、行商、西方商人、消费者等多环节参与者，通过筛选、传递、建构茶叶知识形成的社会产物。

• 贸易全程中，“口感”并非关键因素，真正决定其生命力的是：物质属性适配市场需求、价格符合消费能力、知识传播填补产销鸿沟，以及各方在贸易博弈中形成的动态平衡。
