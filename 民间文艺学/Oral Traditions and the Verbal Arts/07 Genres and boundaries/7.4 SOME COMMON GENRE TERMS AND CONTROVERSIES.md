#### 7.4 SOME COMMON GENRE TERMS AND CONTROVERSIES
####  翻译结果：一些常见的文类术语及相关争议

A huge number of genre-terms have been developed in both popular and scholarly usage, many by now with a quasi-technical status. Whatever your general reaction to the problems of classification, some of these will almost certainly be terms which you will either consider using yourself or meet in others’ writings. A selection only is discussed below, as these can illustrate the controversies which can lie behind even the more innocent-sounding words.

在通俗和学术用法中，已发展出大量文类术语，其中许多如今已具有准专业地位。无论你对分类问题的总体态度如何，你几乎肯定会考虑使用其中一些术语，或在他人著作中遇到它们。下文仅讨论部分术语，这些术语可例证即便看似无争议的词汇背后也可能存在的争议。

A general problem underlying all these terms is that even when scholars endeavour to prescribe standard definitions, these are seldom universally followed. Thus ‘myth’, by some confined to only certain types of narrative (see below), is often used in popular collections to refer to any or all unwritten verbal forms. The same sometimes applies to ‘legends’, ‘sagas’, ‘folktale’ or even ‘epic’-titles of books and articles can be misleading!

所有这些术语背后存在一个普遍问题：即便学者试图制定标准定义，这些定义也很少被普遍遵循。例如，“神话” 被一些人限定为仅指特定类型的叙事（参见下文），但在通俗集子中常被用来指代任何或所有无文字口头形式。“传说”“萨迦”“民间故事” 甚至 “史诗” 也存在类似情况 —— 书籍和文章的标题可能具有误导性！

(A comprehensive bibliography for this huge area is impossible here, but a few starter-not necessarily systematic-references for specific terms are mentioned below; other general references worth consulting include entries in M.Leach 1949 (useful despite its age), Barnouw 1989 (see esp. under ‘Folktale’), Ben-Amos 1976, Bødker 1965 (full list of terms, with further references), Ranke 1977-, and summaries in surveys of folklore or comparative literature such as Dorson 1972a, Brunvand 1986, Chadwick 1932–40, Preminger 1974.)

（此处无法提供这一庞大领域的完整文献目录，但下文会提及一些针对特定术语的入门参考文献 —— 不一定系统；其他值得查阅的一般性参考文献包括利奇（1949，尽管年代久远但仍有用）、巴努瓦（1989，尤其参见 “民间故事” 条目）、本 - 阿莫斯（1976）、博德克（1965，包含完整术语列表及进一步参考文献）、兰克（1977-）中的相关条目，以及民俗学或比较文学概述中的总结，如多森（1972a）、布伦万德（1986）、查德威克（1932–1940）、普雷明格（1974）。）

##### 7.4.1 Myth

There have been many approaches to this emotive term: from philosophers, classical scholars, Jungian analysts, and earlier theorists like Max Müller or Andrew Lang, to the ‘functionalist’ definitions (myth as social ‘charter’) or Lévi-Straussian structuralist interpretations more familiar to anthropologists. This extensive history cannot be rehearsed here (for some initial references and examples see Dundes 1984, P.Maranda 1972, Puhvel 1987), but is worth bearing in mind as overtones from these and similar debates attach themselves variously to the term even when it is apparently used as a transparent and unloaded word.

对于 “神话” 这一富有情感色彩的术语，存在诸多研究路径：从哲学家、古典学者、荣格学派分析师、马克斯・缪勒或安德鲁・兰等早期理论家，到人类学家更熟悉的 “功能主义” 定义（神话作为社会 “宪章”）或列维 - 斯特劳斯的结构主义解读。这一漫长历史无法在此详述（部分入门参考文献和例证参见邓迪斯，1984；马拉达，1972；普维尔，1987），但值得铭记的是，即便该术语看似被用作透明且无偏见的词汇，这些及类似争论的余音也会以不同方式附着其上。

Some attempts have been made to legislate on its usage. One influential definition was laid down by Bascom. This links into Bascom’s classification of ‘prose narratives’ more generally (see Fig. 7.1):

有学者试图对其用法进行规范。巴斯康提出了一个具有影响力的定义，该定义与他对 “散文叙事” 的更广泛分类相关联（参见图 7.1）：

|FORM|BELIEF|TIME|PLACE|ATTITUDE|PRINCIPAL CHARACTERS|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|Myth|Fact|Remote past|Different world: other or earlier|Sacred|Non-human|
|Legend|Fact|Recent past|World of today|Secular or sacred|Human|
|Folktale|Fiction|Any time|Any place|Secular|Human or non-human|

Figure 7.1 Bascom’s three forms of prose narratives (source: Bascom 1965a:5)

|形式|信仰属性|时间维度|空间维度|态度属性|核心角色|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|神话|视为事实|遥远过去|异质世界：其他世界或早期世界|神圣|非人类|
|传说|视为事实|近期过去|当下世界|世俗或神圣|人类|
|民间故事|视为虚构|任何时间|任何地方|世俗|人类或非人类|

图 7.1 巴斯康的三种散文叙事形式（来源：巴斯康，1965a：5）

Myths are prose narratives which, in the society in which they are told, are considered to be truthful accounts of what happened in the remote past. They are accepted on faith; they are taught to be believed; and they can be cited as authority in answer to ignorance, doubt, or disbelief. Myths are the embodiment of dogma; they are usually sacred; and they are often associated with theology and ritual. Their main characters are not usually human beings, but they often have human attributes; they are animals, deities, or culture heroes, whose actions are set in an earlier world, when the earth was different from what it is today, or in another world such as the sky or underworld. Myths account for the origin of the world, of mankind, of death, or for characteristics of birds, animals, geographical features, and the phenomena of nature. They may recount the activities of the deities, their love affairs, their family relationships, their friendships and enmities, their victories and defeats.

(1965a:4)

“神话是散文叙事，在讲述它们的社会中，被视为对遥远过去所发生事件的真实记述。它们基于信仰被接受；人们被教导要相信它们；它们可被引为权威，以回应无知、怀疑或不信。神话是教义的化身；它们通常是神圣的；且常与神学和仪式相关联。其核心角色通常并非人类，但往往具有人类属性；它们是动物、神祇或文化英雄，其行为发生在早期世界（彼时地球与今日不同）或另一世界（如天空或冥界）。神话解释世界、人类、死亡的起源，或鸟类、动物、地理特征及自然现象的特性。它们可能叙述神祇的活动、爱情、家庭关系、友谊与敌意、胜利与失败。”

（1965a：4）

This definition sums up the commonest (if often unstated) connotations of the term, pointing to its arguably key features. It is not always easily applicable however. In some African cultures the boundary between ‘myth’ and ‘legend’ is blurred (Finnegan 1970: 361ff), and the term is also widely used in broader senses. It is easy, but dangerous, to assume that all these features automatically go together, for in practice this is not always so. A story which happens to be about-say-the origin of human beings in some ‘other’ time, cannot be just labelled ‘myth’ and thus assumed to be well known and the basis for sanctioned beliefs; nor are all ritualistic or authoritative narratives (including many modern so-called ‘myths’) necessarily about sacred beings or remote time. ‘Belief’ too is difficult to pin down: what is believed in one sense or context may not be in another, and in any case what is it to ‘believe’? This bundle of associations needs to be approached sceptically and in each specific case checked against the ethnographic findings.

该定义总结了该术语最常见（尽管常未明说）的内涵，指出了其可被论证的核心特征。然而，它并非总能轻易适用。在某些非洲文化中，“神话” 与 “传说” 的界限模糊不清（芬尼根，1970：361 页及以后），且该术语也被广泛用于更广泛的意义。==假定所有这些特征必然同时存在是容易但危险的，因为实际情况并非总是如此==。一个恰好讲述人类在某个 “其他” 时代起源的故事，不能仅被贴上 “神话” 标签就假定其广为人知且是受认可信仰的基础；并非所有仪式性或权威性叙事（包括许多现代所谓的 “神话”）都必然关乎神圣存在或遥远时代。“信仰” 本身也难以界定：在某种意义或语境中被相信的事物，在另一种意义或语境中可能不被相信，而 ==“相信” 本身究竟意味着什么？==我们需要以怀疑的态度对待这一系列关联，并在每个具体案例中对照民族志发现进行验证。

Bascom’s primary focus is on content-suitable for some purposes but not all-so for some it is unsatisfying as downplaying the function of upholding the social order (for some anthropologists once the defining essence of ‘myth’), and the cognitive, psychological and symbolic aspects so emphasised by other analysts. It is still influential, however, and illuminating in its encapsulation of so many commonly-held connotations of the term (for further discussions or examples see, besides references above, Abrahams 1985b, Bremmer 1988, M.Jackson 1968, Malinowski 1948, E.K. Maranda 1973, Ray 1980, Röhrich 1984a, Samuel 1988, Samuel and Thompson 1990, Weigle 1982, 1989, Willis 1981; also references and discussion in 2.4, 8.3.3).

巴斯康的核心关注点是内容 —— 这适用于某些目的但非全部 —— 因此对于一些学者而言，该定义并不令人满意，因为它淡化了神话维护社会秩序的功能（对于某些人类学家而言，这曾是 “神话” 的核心定义要素），以及其他分析师极力强调的认知、心理和象征层面。然而，该定义仍具有影响力，且其对该术语众多普遍内涵的概括具有启发意义（进一步讨论或例证参见上述参考文献之外，还有亚伯拉罕斯，1985b；布雷默，1988；杰克逊，1968；马林诺夫斯基，1948；马拉达，1973；雷，1980；勒里希，1984a；塞缪尔，1988；塞缪尔与汤普森，1990；韦格尔，1982, 1989；威利斯，1981；另见 2.4、8.3.3 节中的参考文献和讨论）。

##### 7.4.2 Legend, folktale, and other narrative forms

Given the many collections of narratives and the importance of narrative in human culture generally, the proliferation of connected terms is scarcely surprising. A few leading or specially controversial terms are picked out here (for further discussion and references see Scullion 1984, Röhrich 1984a, Finnegan 1970 chap. 13, Dégh in Dorson 1972, Ben-Amos 1989a, Rooth 1975, also for narratology generally 8.3.4 below, and 7.4.1 above on ‘myth’).

鉴于叙事作品的大量存在以及叙事在人类文化中的普遍重要性，相关术语的激增并不令人意外。下文挑选出一些主要或特别具争议的术语（进一步讨论和参考文献参见斯卡利恩，1984；勒里希，1984a；芬尼根，1970：第 13 章；多森编著中德格的相关论述，1972；本 - 阿莫斯，1989a；鲁思，1975；叙事学的一般性讨论参见下文 8.3.4 节，“神话” 的相关讨论参见上文 7.4.1 节）。

1. Legend. Usages in the past often implied a distinction from both ‘myth’ and ‘folktale’, again well enunciated in Bascom’s schema (1965a: 4– 5-see Fig. 7.1 above) and with much the same features as his treatment of ‘myth’: widely used and recognised in roughly this sense, but sometimes not fitting detailed ethnographic concepts or practices. Recently the term has taken on new vitality in its application to contemporary and urban legends (Brunvand 1981, D.R.Barnes 1984, Smith 1984, Bennett 1985, Bennett et al. 1987, Bennett and Smith 1988 (esp. Part I), 1989).

2. 传说（Legend）：过去的用法常暗示其与 “神话” 和 “民间故事” 的区分，这一点在巴斯康的分类体系中再次得到明确阐述（1965a：4-5，参见上文图 7.1），且其特征与他对 “神话” 的界定大致相同：该意义被广泛使用和认可，但有时与详细的民族志概念或实践不符。近年来，该术语在指代当代传说和都市传说时焕发出新的活力（布伦万德，1981；巴恩斯，1984；史密斯，1984；贝内特，1985；贝内特等，1987；贝内特与史密斯，1988（尤其第一部分），1989）。

3. Folktale. This is the third component of many classifications, complementary to ‘myth’ and ‘legend’. Bascom defines folktales as ‘prose narratives…regarded as fiction’, with the further characteristics that:

They are not considered as dogma or history, they may or may not have happened, and they are not to be taken seriously… may be set in any time and place…. They have been called ‘nursery tales’ but in many societies they are not restricted to children. They have also been known as ‘fairy tales’ but this is inappropriate both because narratives about fairies are usually regarded as true, and because fairies do not appear in most folktales. Fairies, ogres, and even deities may appear, but folktales usually recount the adventures of animal or human characters.

(Bascom 1965a:4)

2. 民间故事（Folktale）：这是许多分类中的第三类，与 “神话” 和 “传说” 互补。巴斯康将民间故事定义为 “散文叙事…… 被视为虚构”，并具有以下进一步特征：

“它们不被视为教义或历史，可能发生过也可能没有，且不应被当真…… 可设定在任何时间和地点…… 它们曾被称为‘童话’，但这并不恰当 —— 因为关于仙女的叙事通常被视为真实，且大多数民间故事中并不出现仙女。仙女、食人魔甚至神祇可能会出现，但民间故事通常叙述动物或人类角色的冒险经历。”

（巴斯康，1965a：4）

This delineation is often followed (and if only for this reason worth knowing about), again with similar costs, benefits and puzzles as for the complementary terms. It is equally often used more broadly to denote (1) any oral narrative which is not specifically a ‘myth’ or ‘legend’, whether fictional or not and whatever the setting, or (2) all narratives (including myths and legends) regarded as oral, popular, ‘traditional’ or told by ‘the folk’ (a wide and sometimes ambiguous meaning). But whatever its exact referent, the term continues to be popular and over the years has stimulated a huge number of collections and publications (for recent analysis and references see Ben-Amos 1989, also Lüthi 1982).

这一界定常被遵循（仅因此就值得了解），其成本、益处和困惑与互补术语大致相同。该术语也常被更广泛地用于指代：（1）任何并非特指 “神话” 或 “传说” 的口头叙事，无论虚构与否、背景如何；或（2）所有被视为口头、通俗、“传统” 或由 “民众” 讲述的叙事（意义广泛且有时模糊）。但无论其确切指代为何，该术语仍继续流行，多年来催生了大量集子和出版物（近期分析和参考文献参见本 - 阿莫斯，1989；另见吕蒂，1982）。

3. Other narrative terms. Among the many other terms are: saga (sometimes used as an inclusive term for all oral or folk narrative and/or the prose equivalent of epic; sometimes roughly overlapping with legend and/or with the German Sage: see Nicolaisen 1988, Whybray 1987:143ff, Bremmer 1988:6f, Scullion 1984, Oinas 1978); Märchen, fairy tale, housetale (often used more or less interchangeably with ‘folktale’ and subject to the same ambiguities; perhaps now less popular than in the past, at least in English, but see Bettelheim 1976, Bottigheimer 1986b, Holbek 1987, Zipes 1979, 1983; also BenAmos 1989); fable (usually defined as a short narrative about animals or plants, with an explicit moral; for references see Carnes 1985); memorate and the more or less equivalent personal narrative (a focus of increasing interest in keeping with current emphases on individual artistry and experience, see Dégh and Vázsonyi 1974, Stahl and Dorson 1977, Bennett 1984, Personal Narratives Group 1989, Labov 1972: chap. 9, Myerhoff 1980, Samuel and Thompson 1990, Abrahams 1985b).

4. 其他叙事术语（Other narrative terms）：众多其他术语包括：萨迦（saga）—— 有时用作所有口头或民间叙事的总称，和 / 或史诗的散文对应形式；有时与传说（legend）和 / 或德语中的 “Sage”（传说）大致重叠（参见尼科莱森，1988；怀布里，1987：143 页及以后；布雷默，1988：6f；斯卡利恩，1984；奥伊纳斯，1978）；童话（Märchen）、仙子故事（fairy tale）、家常故事（housetale）—— 这些术语常或多或少与 “民间故事（folktale）” 互换使用，存在相同的模糊性；如今或许不如过去流行（至少在英语中是如此），但参见贝特尔海姆（1976）、博廷海默（1986b）、霍尔贝克（1987）、齐佩斯（1979, 1983；另见本 - 阿莫斯，1989）；寓言（fable）—— 通常定义为关于动物或植物的短篇叙事，带有明确寓意（参考文献参见卡恩斯，1985）；亲历叙事（memorate）及大致对应的个人叙事（personal narrative）—— 随着当前对个体艺术性和经验的重视，这一范畴受到越来越多关注（参见德格与瓦松伊，1974；斯塔尔与多森，1977；贝内特，1984；个人叙事研究小组，1989；拉波夫，1972：第 9 章；迈耶霍夫，1980；塞缪尔与汤普森，1990；亚伯拉罕斯，1985b）。

The broader term narrative is now coming back into its own with the increasing interest in analyses cutting through more limited generic divisions and crossing the oral/written boundary (see Bauman 1986, Mitchell 1981, and further discussion and references in 8.3.4, 8.5). Much the same applies to story and fiction (see Bauman 1986, Abrahams 1985b) where, as with narrative, recent studies on verbal art come into closer conjunction with current work in literary and linguistic theory.

“叙事（narrative）” 这一更宽泛的术语如今正重获重视，因为越来越多的分析倾向于突破更狭隘的文类划分，跨越口头 / 书面的界限（参见鲍曼，1986；米切尔，1981；进一步讨论和参考文献参见 8.3.4、8.5 节）。“故事（story）” 和 “虚构作品（fiction）” 亦是如此（参见鲍曼，1986；亚伯拉罕斯，1985b）—— 与 “叙事” 类似，近期关于口头艺术的研究与文学理论和语言学理论的当代研究联系日益紧密。

##### 7.4.3 Epic

This influential term was originally based on classical Greek literature (the exemplars of epic being Homer’s long heroic poems), then extended to arguably similar forms widely found in Eurasia in the past and present and to a lesser extent (though this is debated) elsewhere. The term is usually a noun, denoting-variously-a single self-contained poem; a group or cycle of more, or less, related poems; a poetic tradition. Its adjectival use has broader reference in such phrases as ‘epic moments’, ‘epic tales’, ‘epic quality’, or (as in Olrik 1965) ‘epic laws’. The word is also used of written epics, sometimes labelled ‘secondary’ as against the supposedly ‘primary’ oral forms-the exact dividing line often unclear. All or any of these usages are well established, though their overlaps and differences can be confusing.

“史诗（epic）” 这一具有影响力的术语最初源于古希腊文学（史诗的典范是荷马的长篇英雄诗），随后被扩展至欧亚大陆过去和现在广泛存在的、可被论证为类似的形式，在其他地区也有一定分布（尽管这一点存在争议）。该术语通常用作名词，含义多样：可指一首独立完整的诗；一组或一系列或多或少相关联的诗；一种诗歌传统。其形容词用法在 “史诗时刻（epic moments）”“史诗故事（epic tales）”“史诗特质（epic quality）” 或（如奥尔里克，1965）“史诗法则（epic laws）” 等短语中具有更广泛的指代意义。该词也可用于指代书面史诗，这类史诗有时被称为 “次生” 史诗，以区别于所谓的 “原生” 口头形式 —— 二者的确切界限往往模糊不清。上述所有或任何一种用法都已确立，但它们之间的重叠与差异可能令人困惑。

The relatively accepted criteria in the traditional definition of ‘a long narrative (oral) poem on heroic subjects’ are hard to operationalise in practical terms. How long is ‘long’, for example? Some expect many thousands of lines, others around one to two thousand, others less again, or-as in some Russian scholarship-groupings of shorter poems (see Honko 1988:23). This in turn raises the question of how far an epic has to be a single poem. Here again there are differing views, including the category of ‘cyclic epics’ contrasted with ‘unitary’ or ‘unified’ epics (J.W.Johnson 1980:311).

传统定义中 “关于英雄主题的长篇叙事（口头）诗” 这一相对被接受的标准，在实践中难以操作。例如，“长篇” 究竟多长？有人认为需数千行，有人认为约一两千行，还有人认为更短，或（如部分俄罗斯学术研究）将一组较短的诗归为史诗（参见洪科，1988：23）。这进而引发了一个问题：史诗在多大程度上必须是一首独立的诗？对此同样存在不同观点，包括将 “循环史诗（cyclic epics）” 与 “单一史诗（unitary epics）” 或 “统一史诗（unified epics）” 区分开来（约翰逊，1980：311）。

The ‘verse’ or ‘poetry’ criterion poses problems too. Some texts widely accepted as epic contain passages which by most definitions would be classed as prose (see Finnegan 1977:10). Further, the once-assumed division between ‘prose’ and ‘poetry’ is now being rethought (see 7.2.2 above) and music or performance as well as purely textual qualities sometimes considered. Perhaps we need a new definition of the ‘poetic’ aspects of epic in the context of actual oral delivery. This might mean revising some earlier classifications (see Okpewho 1977:172, 186–7).

“诗体（verse）” 或 “诗歌（poetry）” 这一标准也存在问题。一些被广泛认可为史诗的文本包含按大多数定义应归为散文的段落（参见芬尼根，1977：10）。此外，曾经被假定的 “散文” 与 “诗歌” 的划分如今正被重新审视（参见上文 7.2.2 节），音乐、表演以及纯粹的文本特质有时也被纳入考量。或许我们需要在实际口头表演的语境中，对史诗的 “诗意” 层面给出新的定义。这可能意味着修订一些早期的分类（参见奥克佩沃，1977：172, 186-187）。

There are comparable problems with other elements in the definition. ‘Oral’ is a slippery word (see 1.3.1), and the line between narrative and other poetic forms, particularly panegyric, sometimes hard to draw. Finally, what exactly is ‘heroic’? We may feel we have some intuitive understanding (still based on readings of Homer?) but it is scarcely easy to pin down.

该定义中的其他要素也存在类似问题。“口头（oral）” 是一个难以界定的词（参见 1.3.1 节），叙事与其他诗歌形式（尤其是颂诗）之间的界限有时难以划分。最后，“英雄的（heroic）” 究竟意味着什么？我们可能觉得自己有某种直觉理解（仍基于对荷马的阅读？），但这一概念几乎难以确切界定。

Even the standard definition thus has problems, suggesting that the concept of epic may be essentially polysemous and relative. But to this has to be added further controversies and political overtones. Some date from nineteenth-century evolutionist and Eurocentric models-the assumption that any nation worthy of the name must pass through a heroic stage with an epic to match. Its role in nationalist movements intensified this. There was ‘a feeling abroad’, as Hatto well puts it, ‘that every European nation should have its epic, a feeling so strong that where there was none an epic was, if not invented, nevertheless somehow got together and produced’ (Hatto 1980:18)-a process not without parallels in twentieth-century post-colonial nation-building. Because of this background, even the most innocuous-seeming definition or attribution of ‘epic’ can be emotive. (Among the huge literature see specially, besides references already cited, Oinas 1978, Koljević 1980, Opland 1986, Foley 1990a; for the controversy over African epic, which illustrates the problems above, see Finnegan 1970:108–10, with responses or further comments in Biebuyck 1976, Okpewho 1977, 1979, J.W. Johnson 1980, Ben-Amos 1983, Seydou 1982, Opland 1983:143ff.)

因此，即便是标准定义也存在问题，这表明史诗概念本质上可能是多义且相对的。除此之外，还存在进一步的争议和政治内涵。一些争议源于 19 世纪的进化论和欧洲中心主义模型 —— 该模型假定任何名副其实的民族都必须经历一个英雄时代，并拥有一部相应的史诗。史诗在民族主义运动中的作用加剧了这一观念。正如哈托所言，“当时有一种普遍的看法，即每个欧洲民族都应该拥有自己的史诗，这种看法如此强烈，以至于在没有史诗的地方，人们即便没有凭空创造，也会以某种方式拼凑出一部史诗”（哈托，1980：18）—— 这一过程在 20 世纪后殖民国家的建国过程中并非没有类似情况。由于这一背景，即便是看似最无争议的史诗定义或归属划分，也可能带有情感色彩。（相关海量文献中，除上述参考文献外，特别参见奥伊纳斯，1978；科尔耶维奇，1980；奥普兰德，1986；福利，1990a；关于非洲史诗的争议（可例证上述问题），参见芬尼根，1970：108-110，相关回应或进一步评论参见比布伊克，1976；奥克佩沃，1977, 1979；约翰逊，1980；本 - 阿莫斯，1983；塞杜，1982；奥普兰德，1983：143 页及以后。）

##### 7.4.4 Riddles, proverbs and conversational genres

There is a vast literature on proverbs and riddles. As so often, there are disputes about definition and interpretation, often useful in pointing to features that might otherwise be neglected. (On riddles see Abrahams and Dundes in Dorson 1972a, Finnegan 1970: 426ff, Pepicello and Green 1984, Haring 1985; on proverbs Abrahams in Dorson 1972a, Kuusi 1972, Mieder and Dundes 1981, Mieder 1982, Briggs 1988:101ff.) In recent years there has been some reaction against the compiling of yet more and more lists of proverbs, etc., in favour of more analytic study of their usage, context and interpretation.

关于谚语（proverbs）和谜语（riddles）的文献数量庞大。与往常一样，其定义和解读存在争议，这些争议往往有助于揭示可能被忽视的特征（关于谜语，参见多森编著中亚伯拉罕斯与邓迪斯的相关论述，1972a；芬尼根，1970：426 页及以后；佩皮切洛与格林，1984；哈林，1985；关于谚语，参见多森编著中亚伯拉罕斯的相关论述，1972a；库西，1972；米德与邓迪斯，1981；米德，1982；布里格斯，1988：101 页及以后）。近年来，出现了一种反对一味编纂更多谚语列表等做法的趋势，转而支持对其用法、语境和解读进行更具分析性的研究。

Other conversational genres, like word play, greetings and blessings, have attracted less work, and not yet, it seems, an established vocabulary (on jokes, however, see Briggs 1988:171ff; on conversational genres generally Abrahams 1968b, Tannen 1989, also further discussion or examples in Bauman and Sherzer 1974, Sherzer 1983, Kratz 1989, Finnegan 1969b, 1970:466f).

其他言语文类（conversational genres），如文字游戏（word play）、问候语（greetings）和祝福语（blessings），受到的研究较少，似乎尚未形成固定的术语体系（但关于笑话，参见布里格斯，1988：171 页及以后；关于言语文类的一般性讨论，参见亚伯拉罕斯，1968b；坦嫩，1989；进一步讨论或例证参见鲍曼与谢尔泽，1974；谢尔泽，1983；克拉茨，1989；芬尼根，1969b，1970：466f）。

#### 7.4.6 Cross-generic analyses

Some scholars prefer to treat all the genres within a community together rather than separately. This may involve some genre differentiation, but means that all forms can be analysed together and compared or contrasted within the same framework (this may include visual or material as well as primarily verbal forms). As illustrated in Fig. 7.2, this approach can lead to new insights into the relation between local categories, based on the ethnographic evidence, but sometimes of wider comparative significance too (see also Sherzer 1974, 1990, Howell 1986, Briggs 1988, Classic 1982, Gossen 1974, Harries 1977, Tedlock and Tedlock 1986, Ben-Amos 1987, Feld 1988, Moyo 1986).

部分学者倾向于将一个社群内的所有文类放在一起研究，而非分开处理。这可能涉及一定的文类区分，但意味着所有形式都能在同一框架内被共同分析、比较或对比（这可能包括视觉或物质形式，以及主要的口头形式）。如图 7.2 所示，这种方法基于民族志证据，能让人对本土范畴之间的关系产生新的洞见，但有时也具有更广泛的比较意义（另见谢尔泽，1974, 1990；豪厄尔，1986；布里格斯，1988；克莱西克，1982；戈森，1974；哈里斯，1977；特德洛克夫妇，1986；本 - 阿莫斯，1987；费尔德，1988；莫约，1986）。
