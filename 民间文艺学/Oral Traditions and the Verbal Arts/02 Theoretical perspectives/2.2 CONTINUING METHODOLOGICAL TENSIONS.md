> Certain recurrent debates cut across the more specific theories. Some relate to general controversies within social research—already familiar ground for anthropologists—others link more directly to oral traditions and verbal arts. They include:

### 2.2 持续的研究方法张力

在更为具体的理论之上，还存在一些反复出现的广泛性争论。部分源于社会研究中普遍存在的理论分歧——人类学家对此早已熟悉；另一些则更直接地与口头传统和言语艺术的研究相关。这些争论主要包括以下几个方面：

> 1 The role of comparison and generalisation as against investigating uniqueness and specificity. Complementing the long tradition of classification and generalisation there is now a counter-trend towards exploring people's own views and artistry rather than analysing through outsiders' categories, but the debate continues about how far researchers can or should look for underlying generalities, how far for culture-specific conventions or individual events/personalities.

**比较与概括的作用** —— 与研究独特性和特殊性的关系。  
与长期以来强调分类与概括的学术传统并行的，是一种越来越重视人们自身观点与艺术表达的趋势，而不是仅仅依赖外部研究者的分析范畴。然而，学界仍在讨论研究者应当或能够在多大程度上寻求普遍性的规律，又应在多大程度上关注文化特有的规范或个体性的事件与人格。

> 2 The collection and analysis of texts as against intensive fieldwork on social processes. In some circles there has been a marked shift towards the latter, but the former is still important, particularly in countries with large archive collections.

**文本的收集与分析** —— 与对社会过程的深入田野研究的对立。  
在一些学术圈中，研究重心明显转向了社会过程的实地观察，但文本收集与分析仍然至关重要，尤其是在拥有大量档案资料的国家。

> 3 The nature of the essential subject matter: verbal texts? contexts? performance(s)? the dynamics of a particular occasion? There are disagreements too about how far one can isolate any one of these elements, and in particular about the nature and status of texts (see 1. 4).

**研究对象的本质问题** —— 是语言文本？语境？表演？还是特定事件的动态？  
学者之间还存在分歧：是否可以将这些要素彼此区分？尤其是关于“文本”的性质与地位问题（见 1.4 节）。

> 4 Who researches and on whom? These questions often turn on a contrast between researchers as outsiders and/or superiors as opposed to local scholars and native speakers. Similar questions also arise in 'own culture' research: this too is sometimes undertaken in a spirit of looking down on (sometimes romanticising) certain groups or activities. There is also the question of whether a foreign culture or one's own should be the object of study. Those labelling themselves folklorists in the past mainly concentrated on the second, anthropologists on the first, but such distinctions are becoming more blurred.

**谁在研究，研究的对象是谁？**  
这些问题往往涉及研究者与被研究者之间的身份与权力差异——外来者或优越者与本地学者或母语使用者之间的对比。类似的疑问也存在于“本土文化研究”中：研究者有时以俯视（甚至浪漫化）的眼光看待特定群体或活动。同时，也存在应研究外来文化还是自身文化的争论。过去，自称为“民俗学家”的学者多专注于后者，而人类学家则主要研究前者，但这种区分正变得越来越模糊。

> Whatever your specific methodology these more general debates will continue to arise. In critiquing both your own analyses and those of others it can be illuminating to complement your own views by sometimes looking again at the other side of these ultimately unresolvable tensions.

无论采用何种具体方法，这些更为普遍的争论都将不断出现。无论是在反思自己的分析，还是批评他人的研究时，适时地从这些看似无法最终调和的张力的“另一面”重新审视问题，往往能够带来新的启发。
