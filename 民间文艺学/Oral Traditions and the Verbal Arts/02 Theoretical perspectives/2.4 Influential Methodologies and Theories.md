
### **2.4 INFLUENTIAL METHODOLOGIES AND THEORIES**（2.4 主要的方法与理论）

> This section provides a summary checklist of some leading perspectives— inevitably selective and personal, though I have tried to be even-handed. The emphasis is on their specific implications for the study of oral arts and traditions (rather than more general accounts), with particular stress on the kinds of questions they focus on, together with some guidance to further sources. Theoretical viewpoints can be divided up in different ways: the following provides merely one point of departure.

本节提供若干主要研究视角的概要清单——难免带有选择性和个人倾向，但我已尽力保持平衡。重点在于这些方法对**口头艺术与传统研究**的具体启示（而非一般性理论概述），特别强调它们所关注的问题类型，并附带一些进一步的参考方向。理论视角的划分方式多种多样，下文仅提供一种出发点。

#### **2.4.1 Some nineteenth- and early twentieth-century theories** 十九世纪与二十世纪早期的理论

> Earlier approaches were mostly designed to elucidate the history of particular forms (principally narrative) in terms of origins, diffusion or evolution. Amidst the many variants around this theme the following are worth listing briefly, for though widely regarded as out-dated in their original form, they still sometimes surface or are disinterred for re-analysis into more up-to-date theories.

早期研究方法大多旨在通过“起源”“传播”或“演化”来阐明特定形式（主要是叙事）的历史。在这一主题的诸多变体中，下列几种尤为值得简要列出——虽然它们在原始形态上已被视为过时，但有时仍被重新挖掘或转化为现代理论的一部分。

> 1 Development and deterioration theories: fairy tales are broken-down myths, sunk down from higher levels to that of children and/or of lower classes; epics developed from composite folksongs; and similar theories (see Dundes 1969, Holbek 1987: chaps 1–2).  
> 2 Andrew Lang's 'anthropological view': forms like wonder tales originated in a period when what now looks irrational would have seemed, at that primitive stage of savagery, to have been intelligible; they were then passed down through the generations.  
> 3 Nature mythology: stories were essentially about—and derived from—the forces of nature, specially the sun (this also linked with Max Müller's 'disease of language' theory and Indo-European mythology).  
> 4 Contending theories of origin: monogenesis as against polygenesis of tales (the former often taking India as the diffusion centre); or the 'communal' and, as it were, unconscious creation of ballads and other 'folklore', as against creation by single great individuals (see Holbek 1987:29ff).  
> 5 Developmental theories of genres, linking them to particular stages of society, as in the idea that epic comes first in national development or is associated with a 'heroic' stage (see Chadwick 1932–40, Finnegan 1977:266ff); also in some Eastern European analyses with roots in Marxist-evolutionist theory.  
> 6 Myth-ritual theories: myths (and forms like hero narratives) originate in and from rituals. This was in the past often linked to concepts of 'survivals' and Frazerian anthropology, but has also been extended into more modern forms (see discussion in Okpewho 1983:45ff).
> (For discussion and references on the above see Bausinger 1968, Brunvand 1976: chap. 1, Cocchiara 1981, Dorson 1968, S.Thompson 1946, Toelken 1979:12ff, Finnegan 1969a, Dundes 1965, Bauman 1989c.)

1. **发展与退化理论**：认为童话是“破碎的神话”，从更高层次退化到儿童或下层阶级的层面；史诗由复合民歌演变而来，等等（见 Dundes 1969；Holbek 1987，第 1–2 章）。
2. **安德鲁·兰（Andrew Lang）的“人类学观点”**：奇幻故事起源于一个原始的“野蛮”阶段——当时那些如今看来不合理的事物在当时是合乎逻辑的；这些故事随后被代代相传。
3. **自然神话学派**：认为故事本质上描写自然力量，尤其是太阳的作用——此理论与马克斯·缪勒（Max Müller）的“语言疾病论”及印欧神话研究相关。
4. **起源争论理论**：主张故事是单一起源（单源论）还是多重起源（多源论）；前者常以印度为传播中心；亦有“共同体创作”理论——认为民谣等是集体无意识创作，而非个体天才的产物（见 Holbek 1987:29ff）。
5. **体裁发展理论**：认为文学体裁与社会阶段相连，如史诗出现在国家发展初期或“英雄时代”（见 Chadwick 1932–40；Finnegan 1977:266ff），也见于一些植根于马克思主义-进化论的东欧分析。
6. **神话—仪式理论**：主张神话（及英雄叙事）源于仪式实践。此观点早期常与“文化残存”概念及弗雷泽学派人类学相关，但后亦被延伸至更现代的形式（见 Okpewho 1983:45ff）。
（进一步讨论与参考见 Bausinger 1968；Brunvand 1976 第 1 章；Cocchiara 1981；Dorson 1968；S. Thompson 1946；Toelken 1979:12ff；Finnegan 1969a；Dundes 1965；Bauman 1989c。）

#### **2.4.2 'Finnish' historical-geographical method** “芬兰”历史—地理学派

> Partly a reaction against the kind of generalised speculations listed above, this attempted instead to use scientific and empirical methods to classify and analyse the vast body of material that had been collected. Since any given tale was assumed to have originated in the distant past, then travelled outward through a series of variants, the aim was to discover the origin and _Urform_, then track its diffusion. The method consisted in

该方法部分是对上述广义推测的反应，试图以科学与实证方法来分类并分析大量收集到的资料。它假设每个故事都有遥远的起源，并通过一系列变体传播，其目标是发现最初形态（Urform）及其扩散路径。其操作方法包括：

>> assembling as complete a list of versions as possible, in dividing the story into a series of episodes, in minutely comparing each episode's variations as found in the whole body of texts to establish a prototypic original, and in deducing from this comparison the story's wanderings. (Taylor 1928:481–2)

“尽可能收集完整的版本清单，将故事划分为若干情节单元，精细比较各文本中每一单元的差异，以确立原型版本，并据此推断故事的传播历程。”  
——Taylor 1928:481–2

> The main emphases and questions were:
> 1 A search for the archetypal form of a given tale, defined in terms of its content or plot (pictured as _the 'basic' tale_, over and above its particular tellings).  
> 2 Questions about a tale's origin and its later travels in space and time, the aim being to create its life history' (the history being that of the _tale itself_ rather than its relations to specific historical contexts).  
> 3 As a corollary, little or no interest in questions of performance, local meaning, artistry, social conditions, or stylistic details.  
> 4 A stress on classification and typology (part of the 'scientific' approach); this mostly assumed stable and 'pure' tale-types and genres, ultimately resulting in the tale-type and motif-indexes.  
> 5 A focus on narrative texts, usually under the title of 'tales' (and related sub-categories), very occasionally extended to other genres.  
> 6 Initially mainly about Indo-European forms (still the main focus); later more interest in tales from elsewhere.  
> 7 A dominant (though not exclusive) interest in library and archive-based research rather than intensive fieldwork.

主要研究重点包括：
1. **寻找原型**：定义为故事内容或情节的“基本形式”，超越具体表述。
2. **起源与传播路径**：研究故事在时间与空间中的“生命史”（关注故事自身的历史而非其社会语境）。
3. **对表演与社会语境兴趣甚少**：很少考虑艺术性、社会条件或风格细节。
4. **强调分类与类型学**：假设故事类型与体裁相对稳定、纯粹，进而发展出“故事类型索引”和“主题索引”。
5. **聚焦叙事文本**：主要研究“故事”及相关子类，极少扩展至其他体裁。
6. **研究对象多为印欧语系故事**，后期才扩大至其他地区。
7. **偏重档案与文献研究**：较少进行田野调查。

> Though many scholars (including Finnish folklorists) are now less interested in such questions, there has recently been some resurgence of interest in these methods through narratological and other questions about the relation between 'text' and 'narration' or the status of 'story'. Overall this long-dominant approach has resulted in massive compilations and collections of texts.

尽管今日包括芬兰学者在内的研究者对此兴趣减弱，但近年来==由于叙事学等新问题（如“文本”与“叙述”的关系）==而出现复兴趋势。总体而言，这一方法促成了大量的文本汇编与分类工作。

(For further discussions or references see Azzolina 1987, Brunvand 1976:14ff, Finnegan 1970:320ff, Georges 1983, 1986, Goldberg 1984, Holbek 1987:31ff, 242ff, Honko 1985, Jason 1970, Krohn 1971, Rosenberg and Smith 1974, Utley 1978, Zumwalt 1988: 107ff, also B.H.Smith 1981 and 8.2 below.)

#### **2.4.3 Comparative philology and mythology** 比较语言学与神话学

> There is a long-established interest in Indo-European origins and connections, based in the comparative work of such scholars as William Jones or Max Müller. Indo-European roots have been seen as the basis not only for the classical civilizations of Greece and Rome but for Western culture as a whole, an approach still popular in recent writings. Dumézil's works on Indo-European mythology stand out particularly:

长期以来，学界对印欧语系的起源与联系保持兴趣，奠基者包括威廉·琼斯（William Jones）与马克斯·缪勒（Max Müller）。印欧语系被视为不仅孕育了希腊与罗马文明，也是整个西方文化的根基——这一思路至今仍常见。杜梅齐尔（Georges Dumézil）关于印欧神话的研究尤具影响力，他将其描述为：

> A series of studies devoted to a comparative exploration of the religion of Indo-European peoples, to the ideas those peoples had formed of human and divine society, and to their social and cosmic hierarchy (Dumézil 1988:9)

“一系列比较性研究，旨在探究印欧民族的宗教、他们关于人类与神界社会的观念，以及他们的社会与宇宙等级秩序。”  
——Dumézil 1988:9

> Dumézil's writings—a huge series of volumes—have been immensely influential among certain scholars: 'a landmark fusion of philological, literary, and anthropological methods in the study of belief systems and traditional narrative' (Nagy 1986:38). The main interests are:
> 1 The exploration of basic Indo-European patterns of social organisation and symbolism, together with their associated rituals.  
> 2 The way these are related to—and arguably underly and shape—mythology across the Indo-European areas, including myths which on the surface have become elaborated in different ways.  
> 3 (Perhaps) the extension of similar methods outside the Indo-European area.

杜梅齐尔的庞著影响深远，被认为是“语言学、文学与人类学方法在信仰体系与传统叙事研究中的里程碑式融合”（Nagy 1986:38）。其核心兴趣包括：
1. 探索印欧社会组织与象征模式及其相关仪式；
2. 分析这些模式如何在不同印欧神话中体现并塑造其结构；
3. （或许）将相似的方法扩展至非印欧地区。

(For further discussion and exemplification see Dumézil 1970, 1973, 1983, 1986, 1988 (only a small selection of Dumézil's vast output), also Belmont 1983, Oosten 1985, Puhvel 1987, Rivière 1979.)

#### **2.4.4 Psychological analyses** 心理学分析

> Psychological approaches of various kinds are well recognised in the study of literature and art (see Kiell 1982, Eagleton 1983: chap. 5), and have also been taken up in recent work within anthropology. It is not therefore surprising to find them also applied to oral forms. There is a vast range, from earlier (and sometimes still influential) evolutionist versions, and Freudian or Jungian interpretations by both scholars and therapists, to more culturally specific analyses by anthropologists or oral historians. The concepts of 'myth' and 'mythic' are usually prominent, as are dreams and fantasies, sometimes now combined with structuralist, Marxist or feminist approaches. Particular points include:

> 1 Theories linking psychic and human/social development, often expressed in myths.  
> 2 Interest in such questions as the psychological significance of fantasy and of symbolic representations (male-female relations or parent-child antagonisms in fairy tales, for example), the social psychological roles of story-telling, or the psychic power of myth in shaping action or imagination.  
> 3 Interpretations of the content of folktales and myths in terms of sexual symbolism (psychoanalytic) or universal archetypes (Jungian).  
> 4 The interpretation of fairy tales and other narratives as a potential schema for an individual's life course.  
> 5 The relation between dreams and narratives as expressions of unconscious feelings and/or symbolic structures.  
> 6 The significance of myth for universal archetypes, for the course of human development, and/or for individual or group identity.  
> 7 The development and significance of the emotions, a subject in which anthropologists have recently started to take more interest.

在文学与艺术研究领域，各种心理学研究取向已获得广泛认可（参见Kiell 1982, Eagleton 1983: 第5章），近年来也逐渐被人类学研究所采纳。因此，这些理论方法被运用于口头形式的研究也就不足为奇。相关研究范畴极为广泛——从早期（某些至今仍具影响力）进化论流派的阐释，到学者与心理治疗师提出的弗洛伊德式或荣格式解析，乃至人类学家或口述历史学者进行的文化特异性分析。其中"神话"与"神话性"概念，以及梦境与幻想主题往往占据核心地位，这些概念时下常与结构主义、马克思主义或女性主义理论相结合。具体研究方向包括：
1. **心理发展与社会发展的关联**：常通过神话表现。
2. **幻想与象征的心理意义**：如童话中父母—子女、男女关系的象征。
3. **精神分析与原型论解释**：分别关注性象征与普遍原型。
4. **将叙事视为人生进程的潜在模型**。
5. **梦境与叙事的关系**：均为潜意识或象征结构的表达。
6. **神话对个体与集体认同的心理作用**。
7. **情感的形成与社会意义**：此主题正引起人类学更多兴趣。

(For further examples or discussion, see Holbek 1987:259–322, Brunvand 1986:28, 34 (and references there), Dundes 1965:88ff, 1982:200ff, 1980, 1987, Limón and Young 1986:448–9; also for further examples: Campbell 1959–68, Hobbs 1987, von Franz 1972a, b, Jung and Kerenyi 1963, Grolnick 1986, Schwartz 1956, Lüthi 1982: esp. 116ff, Edmunds and Dundes 1983, Bettelheim 1976, Dieckmann 1986, Fischer 1963, Weigle 1982, 1989; on emotions, Lutz and White 1986, Lutz and Abu-Lughod 1990; on dreams Tedlock 1987, Descola 1989; on recent approaches in oral history Samuel and Thompson 1990.)

#### **2.4.5 Functionalist and 'reflection' approaches** 功能主义与“反映”理论

> A cluster of more sociological approaches focus round differing assumptions about how art forms, oral traditions, etc., are related to society. These reject explanations in terms of either individual personality or of origins, in favour of more synchronic and socially-oriented questions. In addition to explicitly Marxist approaches (see 2.4.6) the main forms are:

一系列社会学取向关注艺术与社会的关系，拒绝从个人心理或起源角度解释，而提出更具社会性的同步分析。

**'Reflection' theories “反映”理论**

> There is something initially attractive about the model of a one-to-one relation between art/literature/oral tradition on the one hand and 'society' (however defined) on the other. The resultant 'reflection view of art' is sometimes classed as one distinctive theoretical approach (Albrecht 1954, Watt 1964, Finnegan 1977:262ff). Detailed analysis of specifies however tends to disabuse researchers of simplistic and literal reflection ideas (compare Poyatos 1988: xiiff with 327ff on 'literary anthropology', moving from 'reflection' to complexity). Such views, however, still sometimes appeal to researchers who assume that unwritten 'tales' or 'folksongs' are somehow more closely reflective of the 'community' than western 'individually inspired' literature.

艺术/文学/口头传统与"社会"（无论其如何定义）之间存在一一对应关系的模式，初看颇具吸引力。这种"艺术反映论"有时被视作一种独特的理论取向（参见Albrecht 1954, Watt 1964, Finnegan 1977:262及后续）。然而对具体案例的深入分析往往会让研究者摒弃这种简单机械的反映观念（对比Poyatos 1988:序章与327页后关于"文学人类学"的论述，可见从"反映"到复杂认知的演进）。尽管如此，仍有一些研究者倾向于认为，未经过文字记录的"故事"或"民谣"相较于西方"个人灵感创作"的文学，能更直接地反映"社群"面貌。

**'Functionalism 功能主义'**

> The functionalist approach dominant in British anthropology in the mid-twentieth century, particularly the 'structural-functional' form emphasising stability and homogeneity, had direct implications for the study of oral arts and traditions in that period. These included:

二十世纪中期的英国人类学强调结构—功能主义，注重稳定与一致性，对口头艺术研究影响深远。主要特征包括：

> 1 A focus on the function of 'myth' (the favoured term) in upholding the status quo. The concept of a 'mythical charter' was particularly prominent, usually as an origin myth sanctioning the current ruler's position (a concept derived from Malinowski 1948—where in fact he takes a wider view of narrative and its artistry).  
> 2 Little interest in other forms of verbal art as 'only' of artistic significance, hence marginal for social structure, with the possible exception of moral tales for educating children or reinforcing socially sanctioned norms.  
> 3 A playing down of local meanings, artistry, individual creativity, or conflicting interpretations in favour of the generalised function of upholding the overall social order.  
> 4 Usually an emphasis on the 'traditional' order with little interest in diachronic analysis or 'new' forms. (On specific implications for the study of oral art/tradition see Finnegan 1969a, 1970:330ff, Okpewho 1983:20ff).

1. 关注“神话”（为首选用语）在维持既有秩序中的功能。“神话宪章”这一概念尤为突出，通常作为一种起源神话，用以为当权者的地位提供认证（该概念源自 Malinowski 1948 —— 虽然事实上他对叙事及其艺术性持更为广泛的看法）。
2. 对其他形式的口头艺术仅被视为“纯粹”艺术性而兴趣不大，因此在社会结构中被边缘化，可能的例外是用于教化儿童或强化社会认可规范的道德故事。
3. 把地方意义、艺术性、个体创造性或相互冲突的解释淡化，以强调维护整体社会秩序的概括性功能。
4. 通常强调“传统”秩序，对历时分析或“新”形式兴趣不大。（关于此对口头艺术／传统研究的具体影响，参见 Finnegan 1969a、1970:330ff、Okpewho 1983:20ff。）

**Functions and functional inter-relationships more generally 功能与功能性相互关系的一般性论述**  

> A more common view now is that verbal arts and traditions both mould and are moulded by the culture (as well as by interested groups or individuals within it) and that the functions fulfilled by art in society are likely to be multiplex rather than single. To that extent perhaps all social scientific approaches are functionalist in the sense of relating art and tradition to the wider social arrangements of the society in which they are practised. But in recent work a wider set of possible functions and roles are emphasised (see 6.3) than the limited ones developed in the specific structural-functional phase of anthropology. (On sociological approaches to art more generally see also Wilson 1964, Wolff 1983, Zolberg 1990.)

如今更普遍的观点是：言语艺术与传统既塑造文化，也被文化所塑造（同样也受其内部相关群体或个人的影响），而艺术在社会中所履行的功能更可能是多重的，而非单一的。在这个意义上，也许所有社会科学的取向在某种程度上都带有功能主义倾向——即把艺术与传统联系到其所实践社会更广泛的社会安排之中。但近来的研究强调了比人类学在特定结构—功能阶段所提出的有限功能更多样的可能功能与角色（参见 6.3）。（关于更一般的社会学艺术研究方法，另见 Wilson 1964、Wolff 1983、Zolberg 1990。）

#### **2.4.6 Marxist perspectives** 马克思主义视角

> The multiplicity of approaches influenced or deemed to be influenced by insights ultimately drawing their inspiration from Marx and his followers are too extensive to present or even summarise here. It should be said however that despite their relative absence in traditional 'folklore' studies they are as applicable to the subject matter of this volume as any other theoretical approach. This body of writing is yet another resource to draw on, and—the other way round—there is a set of topics here to which Marxist writers could pay greater attention.

那些受马克思及其追随者思想影响或被认为受其影响的多种方法繁多，浩如烟海，无法在此详尽陈述或完全概括。不过应当指出，尽管马克思主义方法在传统“民俗学”研究中相对少见，但它们对本书主题的适用性与其他任何理论方法一样有价值。这类著述是可资借鉴的又一资料来源；反过来，口头传统与言语艺术的若干议题也值得马克思主义学者作更多关注。

> The following list gives a simplified impression of a developing and complex set of approaches but can illustrate the kinds of themes round which Marxist writings have focused:

下列要点提供了对这一不断发展且复杂的取向的简化印象，但足以说明马克思主义写作所集中讨论的主题类型：

> 1 The relevance of prevailing social conditions (interpreted largely in economic and political-power terms, often but not always using the terminology of the class struggle). The nature of the relationship between cultural productions and social conditions is a matter for debate: approaches range from cruder theories prevalent in the 30's—art as reflection of social conditions—or the view of 'folklore' as 'a weapon of class conflict' (Sokolov 1971: 15), to the Frankfurt School's concern with the dominant class's incorporation of popular culture through the culture industry, interest in the processes of hegemony and consent by Gramsci and his followers, or interpretations of the way the dominant class imposes meanings while concealing the power on which these are based. Unlike the a-social framework of many historical-geographical or psychological approaches, the processes of social and cultural reproduction are seen as essentially bound in with current social conditions.

1. 当下社会条件的相关性（主要以经济和政治权力角度来解释，常常但并非总是使用阶级斗争的术语）。文化生产与社会条件之间关系的性质是争论的焦点：相关方法从 1930 年代流行的粗糙理论——艺术作为社会条件的反映，或将“民俗”视为“阶级斗争的武器”（Sokolov 1971:15）——到法兰克福学派关注统治阶级通过文化工业对大众文化的同化，格兰西及其追随者对霸权与同意过程的兴趣，或对统治阶级如何强加意义并掩盖其所依赖的权力机制的种种解释，均有涵盖。与许多历史—地理或心理学方法中非社会化的框架不同，马克思主义视角认为社会与文化再生产过程本质上与当前社会条件紧密相连。

> All the other aspects follow from this, but some points are worth drawing out further:

所有其他方面都由此推导而来，但有些要点值得进一步展开：

> 2 An interest in questions of power and of conflicting (or at any rate differing) interest groups and interpretations: a contrast to the homogeneity pictured in functionalist or romantic analyses. A sensitivity to multiplicities of meanings or the contested nature of certain interpretations follows on here. This is particularly important among Latin American Marxist writers, who dispute the 'meanings' put onto their popular forms by ruling powers or by researchers from outside dominant cultures.

2. 关注权力问题以及相互冲突（或至少不同）的利益群体与解释：这与功能主义或浪漫主义分析中呈现的单一性形成对照。由此带来了对多重意义或某些解释存在争议性的敏感性。这一点在拉丁美洲的马克思主义学者中特别重要，他们质疑统治力量或外来（主导文化的）研究者强加于本地大众文化形式的“意义”。

> 3 Interpretations of 'meaning' as not in the 'text' itself or in 'free' and independent individual authors, but as related to current social conditions. There are disagreements too: over how far popular songs, 'folktales', etc., should be regarded as merely reflecting class interests, the class struggle, or the incorporation and domination by those in power, how far as expressions of 'counter-cultural' resistance by oppressed or marginal groups, and how far as an area of relative freedom for individual expression outside the power struggle. There are debates between 'optimistic' or 'pessimistic' prognoses for emergent popular expression in the face of western capitalism, state domination or mass culture.

3. 对“意义”的解释并不把它视为仅存在于文本本身或存在于“自由”且独立的个体作者中，而是将其与当前社会条件相关联。对此亦有争议：关于流行歌曲、民间故事等应在多大程度上被视为仅仅反映阶级利益与阶级斗争、或是为统治者所同化与支配，抑或在多大程度上可被视为被压迫或边缘群体的“反文化”抵抗表达，或作为个体在权力斗争之外相对自由表达的领域。针对在西方资本主义、国家统治或大众文化压力下新兴大众表达的前景，存在“乐观”与“悲观”之争。

> 4 Debates over the nature and significance of items defined as 'folklore', 'oral', or 'traditional'. The evolutionary influences within Marxism have sometimes found expression in theories about 'stages' of society as related to particular genres; for example a pre-industrial origin was sometimes posited for fairy tales which thus remain as survivals outside the conditioning of modern industrial society and hence of only marginal interest. Much recent writing however takes all items in current circulation as part of the processes of social and cultural reproduction.

4. 关于被定义为“民俗”“口头”或“传统”的条目性质与意义的争议。马克思主义内部的某些进化主义影响有时体现在关于社会“阶段”与特定体裁相关的理论中；例如有人曾假定童话具有前工业起源，因此被视为脱离现代工业社会条件的残存物，从而仅具边缘意义。然而大量近作则认为，现今流通的所有项属于社会与文化再生产过程的一部分。

> 5 The production and consumption of art forms are proper objects of study (not just their style, meaning or earlier history), leading to such questions as how particular forms are composed and circulated, in what conditions, by and to whom, and in whose interests. Similarly questions arise about the artistic division of labour or the construction of symbols.

5. 艺术形式的生产与消费是应被研究的正当对象（不仅仅是它们的风格、意义或历史），由此产生的问题包括：特定形式如何被创作与流通、在何种条件下、由谁向谁流通、以何种利益为导向。同样，也会出现关于艺术分工或符号建构的问题。

> Since almost everyone is now at least up to a point interested in social behaviour and contexts, and relatively sensitised to the possibility of opposing interests, many of the questions above might no longer be seen as distinctively Marxist but to have become absorbed into the social scientific repertoire in general or woven into other approaches. It is only when such approaches are articulated explicitly (using, perhaps, particular terminologies or citations) that they tend to be labelled 'Marxist' as such.

鉴于当代几乎人人都在某种程度上关注社会行为与语境，并对对立利益的可能性较为敏感，上述许多问题或许已不再被视为明显的“马克思主义”问题，而是被吸纳进一般的社会科学话语或交织入其他方法之中。只有当这些方法被明确表达（或许以特定术语或引用）时，它们才会被贴上“马克思主义”的标签。

> (For discussion or examples see Williams 1973, 1977, Eagleton 1976, and other works on Marxist approaches to literature (sometimes overlapping with feminist, structuralist and post-structuralist approaches), also specific examples or references in Bottomore 1984, Gramsci 1971, S.Hall 1973, 1981, 1985, Hall et al. 1978, Bourdieu 1984, Mattelart 1989; for lively discussion using or extending the 'folklore' terminology see Holbek 1987:391ff, Limón 1983, 1984, Limón and Young 1986:449, W.S.Fox 1980, P.Thompson 1978, also the controversial 'radical' interpretations in Zipes 1979, 1983, 1984; for Soviet work under the label 'folklore'; see Sokolov 1971, Oinas 1984, also the interdisciplinary influence of writers such as Propp 1968, Bakhtin 1968, 1981, 1986.)

#### **2.4.7 Structuralism and post-structuralism** 结构主义与后结构主义

**Structuralism**

> Structuralist approaches have been extremely influential in the study of oral tradition and the verbal arts. Drawing on both Saussurean linguistics and literary theory, they focus, obviously, on the structure of the item being studied, elucidating the laws which underlie it and in a sense constitute its essence. The rules and relationships of human behaviour may, like grammar and syntax in linguistic communication, lie below the level of the actors' consciousness, but in structuralist analysis they form the foundation and the conditions for specific actions and meanings: thus the key topic for study.

结构主义方法在口头传统与言语艺术研究中极具影响力。它们汲取索绪尔式语言学与文学理论的资源，显著地关注被研究对象的结构，阐明其下层规律，并在某种意义上构成其本质。人类行为的规则与关系，类似语言交际中的语法与句法，可能位于行为者意识之下；但在结构主义分析中，这些规则构成了具体行动与意义的基础与条件，因此成为研究的关键议题。

> Of the different strands within structuralism, the relevant ones here are those derived 1) from Propp and the Russian Formalists, primarily on similarities in form—plot, narrative moves, recurrent types of actors; and 2) from Lévi-Strauss, on the logical relationships and binary oppositions within 'myth', these in turn being interpreted as representing universal logical processes of the human mind, mediated through local symbolic interrelationships. Despite the differences, certain broad similarities mark out these approaches:

在结构主义的不同脉络中，此处相关的有两种：一是源自 Propp 与俄国形式主义者，主要关注形式上的相似——情节、叙事推进、反复出现的行动者类型；二是源自 Lévi-Strauss，关注神话内部的逻辑关系与二元对立，并将其解释为通过地方性符号互涉所中介的人类心智之普遍逻辑过程的表现。尽管存在差异，若干广泛的相似点仍可标识这些方法：

> 1 The aim of uncovering the underlying mechanisms/logic/codes/rules: the 'real' structure beneath and behind the contingent surface phenomena (although just _which_ underlying principles are sought may vary: Jason and Segal for example distinguish the four levels of: wording; poetic texture; narrative; and meaning or symbolic component, 1977:3ff).
> 2 An interest in abiding structural rules—usually cross-cultural and universal—rather than the specifics of, say, context, local meanings, history, specific literary works, or individual human actors.
> 3 The constituent units seen as meaningful in relation to each other and within an overall system or code, rather than independently or through correspondence with outside 'reality'.
> 4 Focus on reaching conclusions about 'form' or 'grammar' rather than 'content', particularly in the Proppian versions (though again what is 'form' and what 'content' may not be agreed).
> 5 Interest in a-temporal and scientifically generalisable patterns irrespective of particular dates or versions, and little concern with changing contexts, social interactions or ideologies through time.
> 6 A prime emphasis on narrative (though any form is in principle susceptible to structural analysis).

1. 旨在揭示潜在的机制／逻辑／代码／规则：即掩映于表层偶然现象之下的“真实”结构（不过所寻求的潜在原则可能各异：例如 Jason 与 Segal 区分出 wording、poetic texture、narrative 与 meaning 或 symbolic component 四个层次，1977:3ff）。
2. 关注持久的结构规则——通常被视为跨文化且普遍存在——而非情境、地方意义、历史、具体作品或个体行动者的细节。
3. 把构成单元视为在彼此与整体系统或代码中的关系性意义，而非独立或通过与外在“现实”的对应来理解。
4. 强调对“形式”或“语法”的结论，而非“内容”，尤其在 Propp 式分析中如此（当然何为“形式”、何为“内容”仍可能存在争议）。
5. 关注非时间性的、可被科学地概括的模式，而不拘泥于特定年代或版本，对随时间变化的语境、社会互动或意识形态关注甚少。
6. 以叙事为主要研究对象（尽管原则上任何形式都可进行结构分析）。

> Structuralism has been criticised for de-emphasising local meaning, performance, context, or human interaction, but this has not prevented it from being extremely influential not just in anthropology and folklore but throughout the arts and social sciences. It also sometimes combines with other approaches (specially Marxist, feminist and narratological) as well as providing the foundation and the foil for post-structuralist work. (This is a large and controversial subject on which there has been a great deal of writing. See, for general collections, references or discussion: Maranda and Maranda 1971, Lane 1970, Culler 1975, Dundes 1976, 1982:245f, Holbek 1977b, 1987 (esp. 323ff), Pace 1982, Davis 1986 esp. Parts 2, 6, Todorov 1986, Eagleton 1983: 94ff, 1989; for mainly morphological and formalist analyses: Dundes 1964a, 1965, Propp 1968, Ben-Amos 1989:184, Jason and Segal 1977, Pentikainen and Juurikka 1976, Todorov 1975, 1977, 1986; for Lévi-Strauss and structuralism: Lévi-Strauss 1963, 1967 and the 4-volume Mythologiques 1969–81, Leach 1970a, b. See also 8.3 and references there.)

结构主义因淡化地方意义、表演、语境或人际互动而受到批评，但这并未阻止其在不仅是人类学与民俗学，而且在整个艺术与社会科学领域产生深远影响。结构主义有时也与其他方法结合（尤其是马克思主义、女性主义与叙事学方法），并为后结构主义工作提供基础与对照。（这是一个庞大而具争议的话题，相关著述甚多。有关综合文集、参考或讨论见：Maranda & Maranda 1971、Lane 1970、Culler 1975、Dundes 1976、1982:245f、Holbek 1977b、1987（尤其 323ff）、Pace 1982、Davis 1986（特别是第 2、6 部分）、Todorov 1986、Eagleton 1983:94ff、1989；主要的形态学与形式主义分析见 Dundes 1964a、1965、Propp 1968、Ben-Amos 1989:184、Jason & Segal 1977、Pentikainen & Juurikka 1976、Todorov 1975、1977、1986；关于 Lévi-Strauss 与结构主义见 Lévi-Strauss 1963、1967 以及 Mythologiques 四卷 1969–81、Leach 1970a,b。另见第 8.3 节及其参考文献。）

**Post-structuralism**

> This diverse and argued-over complex of approaches, drawing inspiration from such writers as Derrida and Foucault, both developed from and reacted against structuralist approaches in literature and the social sciences. Relevant points here are their focus on:

这一多元且争论颇多的方法群体，从 Derrida 与 Foucault 等作者汲取灵感，既由结构主义发展而来，又对结构主义提出反应与质疑，尤其在文学与社会科学领域。此处相关要点包括其关注之处：

> 1 Challenging the laws supposedly detected by structuralists as not after all agreed, fixed or value-free.
> 2 Querying the possibility of discovering neutral principles or meanings through scientific investigation; among other reasons 'meaning' is indeterminate and open-ended, shaped by particularistic and fleeting factors, without objective underpinning.
> 3 Arguing that texts are less hard, bounded and self-consistent than had been assumed in other analyses; the process of 'deconstruction' reveals dissonant and unresolved contradictions within texts, undermining their meanings.
> 4 Challenging the assumption that a single text has a clear status in its own right, far less is explicable in terms of its author's individual originality; it can only be understood in the context of other related texts through which the readers/audience make sense of it: 'intertextuality'.

1. 挑战结构主义者所声称发现的规律，认为这些规律并非无可争议、固定或不带价值判断的。
2. 质疑能否通过科学研究发现中立的原则或意义；其理由之一是“意义”本身是不确定且开放的，由特定且短暂的因素塑造，缺乏客观基础。
3. 主张文本并不像其他分析所假定的那样坚固、界限分明或自洽；“解构”过程揭示了文本内部的不和谐与未解的矛盾，从而削弱其既有意义。
4. 挑战这样的假设：单一文本具有明确的自我地位，其含义可由作者的独创性来解释；文本只能在与其他相关文本的关系语境中被理解，读者/受众通过这些关系来赋予其意义——即“互文性”。

> 'Post-structuralism' is even more elusive than other schools of thought, means different things to different writers, and has been much criticised. Some see it as leading to anarchy, or, as Margolis put it of similar moves within aesthetics, 'a drift toward extreme relativism 。on the one hand or the opportunistic imposition of meanings by fiat on the other' (1989:30). However even those supersuaded by this school are sometimes indirectly influenced by its critical approach. Thus, while few studies of oral tradition and arts may directly label themselves 'post-structuralist', recent work has quite often made use of post-structuralist themes such as those of intertextuality, of multiple rather than single meanings, of rejecting a search for objectively-fixed laws, and generally questioning fixed models of text and meaning.

“后结构主义”比其他学派更难以捉摸，对不同作者而言含义各异，且饱受批评。有人认为它导向无政府状态，或如 Margolis 对美学中类似动向所言，“一方面走向极端相对主义，另一方面则可能导致机会主义地以武断方式强加意义”（1989:30）。然而，即便不完全信奉该学派的人也常间接受其批判性方法的影响。因此，尽管鲜有口头传统与艺术研究直接自称“后结构主义”，近来的研究却常采用后结构主义主题，例如互文性、多重而非单一意义、拒绝寻找客观不变规律，以及普遍质疑固定的文本与意义模型。

> (For discussion and references see Harari 1979, Culler 1981, 1982, Eagleton 1983: esp. chap. 4 and 227–8, Attridge et al. 1987, Davis 1986: Part 8, Krupat 1987.)

#### **2.4.8 Narratology** 叙事学

> The narratology school partly developed out of structuralism, with special focus on narrative. This term can be used in a wide sense, eloquently summed up in Barthes' 'Introduction to the structural analysis of narrative':

叙事学派部分源自结构主义，特别关注叙事。该术语可在广义上使用，正如 Barthes 在《叙事的结构分析导论》中所精彩概括的那样：

> There are countless forms of narrative in the world. First of all, there is a prodigious variety of genres, each of which branches out into a variety of media, as if all substances could be relied upon to accommodate man's stories. Among the vehicles of narrative are articulated language, whether oral or written, pictures, still or moving, gestures, and an ordered mixture of all those substances; narrative is present in myth, legend, fables, tales, short stories, epics, history, tragedy, _drame_ [suspense drama], comedy, pantomime, paintings (in Santa Ursula by Carpaccio, for instance), stained-glass windows, movies, local news, conversation. Moreover, in this infinite variety of forms, it is present at all times, in all places, in all societies; indeed narrative starts with the very history of mankind; there is not, there has never been anywhere, any people without narrative; all classes, all human groups, have their stories, and very often those stories are enjoyed by men of different and even opposite cultural backgrounds: narrative remains largely unconcerned with good or bad literature. Like life itself, it is there, international, transl historical, transcultural.  
(1975:37)

世界上存在无数种叙事形式。首先，体裁异常繁多，每一种又可分支为多种媒介，好像任何媒介都可承载人的故事。叙事的载体包括有组织的语言（口头或书面）、静态或动态图像、手势，以及这些媒介有序的混合；叙事存在于神话、传说、寓言、民间故事、短篇、史诗、历史、悲剧、戏剧、喜剧、哑剧、绘画（例如 Carpaccio 的《圣乌尔苏拉》）、彩色玻璃、电影、地方新闻、对话之中。更重要的是，在这无限多样的形式里，叙事无时无处不在；确实，叙事始于人类的历史；任何地方、任何民族从未缺少叙事；所有阶层、所有群体都有他们的故事，而且这些故事常常为不同乃至相反文化背景的人们所喜爱：叙事基本上不以好坏文学为界。正如生活本身，它是国际性的、跨历史的、跨文化的。（Barthes，1975:37）

> Despite this breadth, the central focus is usually on verbally-realised narrative. Narratologists seek to uncover the general patterns irrespective of specific medium or content, and there is now an extensive body of work on written literature. Since narratives form a high proportion of most oral collections, and classic writings in narratology include both Propp's work on fairy tales (1968) and, to a lesser extent, Lévi-Strauss on myth (1963, 1969–81), some interest in oral texts has also always played some part (though arguably not enough, see Parks 1987:527). In one sense narratologists are only saying some of the same old things about narrative. But they are doing so with new vigour, more focused questions and terminology, and within a wider comparative framework.

尽管涵盖面极广，叙事学的中心通常仍是以语言实现的叙事。叙事学者寻求发掘那些不受具体媒介或内容限制的通则模式；目前关于书面文学的相关研究已相当丰富。鉴于叙事在大多数口头资料集中占有较高比重，加之叙事学经典著述既包含 Propp 对童话的形式学研究（1968），也在一定程度上包括列维-斯特劳斯对神话的分析（1963、1969–81），因此对口头文本的关注自始终占有一席之地（尽管有人认为该关注仍嫌不足，参见 Parks 1987:527）。在某种意义上，叙事学者重申了关于叙事的一些传统观点，但他们以新的活力、更集中的问题意向与术语，在更广泛的比较框架下进行探讨。

> It is interesting to locate this approach in the wider study of verbal art forms. Much of the nineteenth- and early twentieth-century work was on stories, with poetic forms only becoming a major focus of interest later (no doubt helped by developments in recording techniques) and conversational genres more recently still. Thus self-consciously innovative work often focused on performance and event, or on the oral-formulaic analysis of mainly poetic texts, avoiding the study of tales as supposedly the domain of the older historical-geographical approach. But narratological work has revived interest in story, sometimes in conjunction with other approaches (as in Abrahams 1985b, Bauman 1986), and has directly or indirectly influenced much recent work on oral narratives (such as Bauman 1986, Calame-Griaule et al. 1980, Cancel 1989, Feld 1989, Shuman 1986, LeRoy 1985, Jason and Segal 1977, see also Paulme 1976, Cosentino 1982, M.Jackson 1982). Since narrative across all media is brought within the same framework, forms once separately classified as being 'oral' or 'African' or 'folk', can take their full part in comparative analysis.

将此取向置于言语艺术更广泛研究中十分有趣。十九世纪与二十世纪早期的大多数研究以故事为主，诗性形式在后期才成为重要焦点（无疑受录音技术发展的推动），会话体类型则更是近年才被重视。因此，那些自觉创新的工作常偏向表演与事件，或对主要为诗性文本的口头—公式化分析，避开将故事研究归入历史—地理学派的传统领域。然而，叙事学的兴起也重新激发了对故事的兴趣，且常与其他方法并用（如 Abrahams 1985b、Bauman 1986），并直接或间接影响了许多近年的口头叙事研究（例如 Bauman 1986、Calame-Griaule 等 1980、Cancel 1989、Feld 1989、Shuman 1986、LeRoy 1985、Jason & Segal 1977，另见 Paulme 1976、Cosentino 1982、M. Jackson 1982）。当跨媒介的叙事被置于同一框架之下，曾被单独标注为“口头的”或“非洲的”或“民俗的”形式，便能在比较分析中充分参与。

> The kinds of topics investigated and analysed include:
> 1 Recurrent patterns in the roles of narrative characters like the villain, hero, trickster.  
> 2 The recurrent moves in, and hence structure of, plots (overlapping with structuralist-morphological analyses, see 2.4.7).  
> 3 The ordering and sequences within narrative, how it is 'framed', and the degree of 'narrativity'.
> 4 The varying 'voices' and viewpoints in the narration.  
> 5 The interaction of 'fiction' and 'event'.  
> 6 The interactive or 'dialogic' relationship with the critic, reader or audience.

研究主题包括：叙事人物角色（如恶棍、英雄、骗子等）中的反复模式；情节的重复推进与由此形成的结构（与结构主义—形态学分析重叠）；叙事中的次序与序列、其“框定”方式，以及所谓“叙事性”的程度；不同的叙述“声音”与视角；“虚构”与“事件”的互动；以及与批评者、读者或受众的互动／对话关系。

> Many of the earlier analyses focused on textual and formal questions but recently there has been more stress on social context, performance or audience, as well as on the roles of narrative, narrator and reader(s)/audience. Some writers approach narration as a performance or speech act, thus linking with discourse and performance theorists (B.H.Smith 1978, 1981; see also 2.4.10 below). Indeed 'narratology' can perhaps now be regarded as encompassing almost any kind of analysis of narrative, developing in a way typical of many approaches in the social sciences from formalist studies to social-context, interpretive, or post-structuralist questions.

> (See also general accounts and references in Scholes and Kellogg 1966, Chatman 1978, Culler 1981: chap. 9, Genette 1980, Mosher 1981, Mitchell 1981, Prince 1982, 1987 (a guide to the burgeoning technical terms), 1989, Jameson 1981; examples or discussions in Bakhtin 1981, Bauman 1986: esp. 5ff, Shuman 1986, Parks 1987; also 8.3.4.)

早期许多分析侧重文本与形式，而近来则更多强调社会语境、表演或受众，以及叙事者、叙述者与读者／观众的角色。一些作者甚至将叙述视为一种表演或言语行为，从而与话语与表演理论相联结（参见 B.H. Smith 1978、1981；另见下文 2.4.10）。可以说，今日的“叙事学”几乎可涵盖任何类型的叙事分析，其发展轨迹类似于社会科学中许多方法从形式主义扩展到社会语境、解释学或后结构主义问题的过程。

**2.4.9 The 'oral theory'**

The so-called 'oral theory' first started from controversies about the nature and composition of the Homeric epics, inspiring Milman Parry's studies of Homeric formulae, followed by fieldwork on South Slavic oral heroic poetry in the 1930's. The results appeared in Albert Lord's enormously influential _The Singer of Tales_ (1960). This demonstrated how songs many thousands of lines long could be composed without writing, by the singer's drawing on a store of formulae and formulaic expressions with no need to prepare a text beforehand: composition and performance were not separate stages but facets of the same act. Some formulae were short phrases fitting a given metrical position, but longer formulaic expressions included runs of several lines, themes, topics and narrative plots. There was no fixed or 'correct' text, as in written literature, for each performance was different and equally authentic: "'an'' original, if not "the'' original' (Lord 1960:101).

The discovery of this 'oral-formulaic' mode of composition was a breakthrough. It was soon applied to other works, notably _Beowulf_ and other early medieval texts, and to the study of oral poetry in general (see Finnegan 1977: chap. 3). The main questions were:

1 How far a given work was 'oral' or 'written' (the 'oral-formulaic' mode being taken as the hallmark of oral composition).  
2 The nature of oral composition and transmission, with a stress on the 'formulaic' mode.  
3 The characteristics of oral as against written literature (the 'oral theory' being taken as a theory of oral literature in general).  
4 The nature of 'tradition', seen as the inherited store of themes and formulae on which the singer draws.

The 'oral theory' has been criticised for its sometimes over-simple oral/written dichotomy, for its dependence on a particular model of composition, and for its emphasis on heroic poetry (see Finnegan 1977, 1988: chaps 1–2). But it has been extremely influential in a number of disciplines, and has been applied to a wide range of forms, from Anglo-Saxon poetry and African praise poetry to modern folk song and the Bible. It has also been developed in a number of directions, including:

1 The 'ethnography of speaking' approach (see 2.4.10 below).  
2 The study of 'oral history' (see 9.2.2).  
3 The 'oral-written' continuum (see 9.1.2).  
4 The comparative study of 'oral literature' and 'oral poetry'.

(For discussion and references see Lord 1960, 1986a, b, 1987, 1991, Foley 1985, 1988, 1990, Finnegan 1977, 1988, J.M.Foley 1986, 1990b, Parks 1986, 1987, Okpewho 1979, 1985, 1990, Niles 1983, Zumwalt 1988: chap. 5, also the journal _Oral Tradition_.)

**2.4.10 Performance approaches**

The 'performance' approach is now extremely influential in the study of oral arts and traditions. It has many strands, but the central focus is on performance as an organising concept. The main points can be summarised as:

1 Performance is seen as the central and definitive focus for artistic oral expression. It is not just an embellishment of pre-existent texts, but the very essence and realisation of the art.  
2 Performance is conceived as an event, with emergent and interactive qualities, not just the enactment of pre-existing texts or rules. It is thus a creative occasion, with the audience playing an active role.  
3 The focus is on the specific and unique performance, not on some generalised and abstract model or average. The text is seen as realised in and through performance, not as something fixed and independent.  
4 The stress is on the process of performance, not just on the product. This includes the performer's skills and the dynamics of the performance as a communicative event.  
5 The ethnographic context is crucial: the performance cannot be understood without knowledge of the cultural and social setting, the participants, the norms and expectations, and the local aesthetics.  
6 The performance is often seen as a mode of communication, with special features of its own (such as heightened language, special formulae, or particular paralinguistic features). This links with the 'ethnography of speaking' school and the study of performance as a specific speech event.  
7 The approach often involves close attention to the details of performance: the use of language, the role of the audience, the performer's gestures and movements, the use of space, the sequencing of events, and the interaction between performer and audience.

The performance approach has been particularly influential in American folklore and sociolinguistics, but has also been taken up in anthropology, literature, and other disciplines. It has led to a greater emphasis on fieldwork, on the recording of actual performances, and on the analysis of performance as a social and cultural event. It has also encouraged a more dynamic and processual view of oral tradition, and a greater sensitivity to the creativity and artistry of performers.

(For further discussion and examples see Bauman 1977, 1986, Bauman and Sherzer 1974, Hymes 1975, 1981, Ben-Amos and Goldstein 1975, Stoeltje 1988, Fine 1984, Schechner 1985, Turner 1982, 1986, also 8.4 below.)

**2.4.11 Ethnopoetics**

Ethnopoetics is a relatively recent development, with close links to performance approaches and to the ethnography of speaking. It focuses on the poetic structures of oral performances, particularly those of non-western and traditionally oral cultures. The main aims are:

1 To represent oral texts in a way that captures their poetic form and performance features, rather than reducing them to prose or to western poetic models.  
2 To analyse the native poetics: the local aesthetic principles, the stylistic conventions, the patterns of repetition, parallelism, and other poetic devices.  
3 To understand the cultural and cognitive dimensions of oral poetry: how it reflects and shapes ways of thinking and cultural values.

Ethnopoetics has been particularly influential in the study of Native American narratives and poetry, but has also been applied to African, Oceanic, and other traditions. It often involves close textual analysis, combined with ethnographic understanding of the cultural context.

(For discussion and examples see Hymes 1981, Tedlock 1983, 1987, Sherzer and Woodbury 1987, Bright 1984, Foley 1988, 1990a, also the journal _Alcheringa/Ethnopoetics_.)

**2.4.12 Feminist approaches**

Feminist perspectives have been increasingly influential in the humanities and social sciences, and have also been applied to the study of oral traditions and verbal arts. The main points include:

1 A focus on women as performers, creators, and audiences: making women visible in areas where they have been overlooked or marginalised.  
2 An interest in women's genres and forms of expression: those specifically associated with women, or particularly used by them.  
3 A critique of androcentric biases in earlier scholarship: the ways in which women have been ignored, stereotyped, or misrepresented in the collection and analysis of oral traditions.  
4 An exploration of gender as a cultural and social construct, and its relation to oral forms: how gender roles and ideologies are reflected, reinforced, or challenged in oral performances.  
5 A concern with the relation between gender and power: how oral forms can be used to express women's subordination, resistance, or empowerment.  
6 An interest in the body and sexuality as expressed in oral forms.  
7 A revaluation of forms and genres traditionally associated with women (such as lullabies, lamentations, or gossip) and a challenge to the hierarchies that have privileged male-associated forms (such as epic or heroic poetry).

Feminist approaches often overlap with Marxist, performance, and post-structuralist perspectives, and have led to a greater awareness of gender as a crucial dimension in the study of oral traditions and verbal arts.

(For discussion and examples see Jordan and Kalčik 1985, Herndon and Ziegler 1990, Lawless 1991, Mills 1991, Radner 1993, Sklar 1991, Titon 1988, also the journal _Women and Folklore_.)

**2.4.13 Summary**

This chapter has provided a brief and selective overview of some of the main theoretical perspectives that have been, or are, influential in the study of oral traditions and verbal arts. The aim has been to illustrate the range of questions that can be asked, and to provide some guidance to further reading. In practice, researchers often draw on more than one approach, and the boundaries between them are not always clear-cut. The choice of theory will depend on the researcher's interests, the nature of the material, and the specific questions being asked. The important point is to be aware of the theoretical assumptions being made, and to use theory as a tool to open up understanding, not as a straitjacket to confine it.
