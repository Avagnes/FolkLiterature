> Anthropologists' interest in oral tradition and verbal art is, no doubt, to understand people's cultural activities and artistries, rather than to build theory. But some knowledge of the existing theoretical and comparative literature opens up greater appreciation of problems and possibilities, whether in developing the most appropriate research strategy, raising previously unconsidered questions, or assessing the work of others. And although anthropologists rightly stress the importance of being open to cultural specifics rather than sticking rigidly to some general 'theory', the conduct of research inevitably depends on _some_ theoretical assumptions—so these are best recognised explicitly.
### 2.1 理论的作用

人类学家之所以关注口头传统与言语艺术，无疑是为了理解人们的文化活动与艺术创造，而不是为了建构抽象理论。然而，对现有理论与比较性文献的了解，能够让研究者更深入地理解研究中的问题与潜在可能——无论是在制定最合适的研究策略、提出以往未曾考虑的问题，还是在评价他人研究成果时，这种理论认识都大有裨益。

尽管人类学家理应强调保持对文化特殊性的开放态度，而非僵化地遵循某种通用“理论”，但研究的开展在实践上必然依赖某些理论假设——因此，最好的做法是明确地意识并加以承认这些假设。

> In this general area, the theoretical controversies extend not only to terminology but also to what the forms or processes under study essentially _are_, and to how or why they can be investigated. The responses to such questions link anthropological research to other disciplines too, from literary criticism to history, philology, and social or linguistic theory. They also tend to cluster round a series of relatively distinctive viewpoints, summarily outlined in this chapter. There is space only for an extremely simplified run-through here, so since any approach (es) taken up for serious development would eventually have to be pursued in greater depth, there are also brief guides to some relevant literature.

在这一领域中，理论上的争论不仅涉及术语的使用，也涉及研究对象的形式或过程“本质上是什么”，以及“如何”或“为什么”可以对它们进行研究。对这些问题的回应，使人类学研究与其他学科产生了关联——例如文学批评、历史学、语言学以及社会或语言理论。

这些不同的观点往往围绕若干相对独特的立场聚集起来，本章将对这些主要观点作简要的概述。由于篇幅有限，这里只能作极度简化的梳理；任何想要深入发展的研究取向，都必须在后续研究中被更深层次地探讨。为此，文中也附带了一些相关文献的简要指引，供进一步阅读参考。
