#### 7.6 FINAL POINTS
#### 最后的观点

The process of classifying, whatever the detailed content, brings us once again to the active and partly arbitrary role of researcher, performer and audiences. Comparative and historical terms have their uses, but so too does investigation of cultural specifics, of the way these do or do not fit with the bundle of connotations associated with cross-cultural terms, of local taxonomies, and of developing formations and transformations around existing expectations. And even when we have settled on the particular classifications that best seem to match the needs of the research and the ethnography there may be yet other strands to divide or unite these categories. The traditional labelling of genres or of other differentiations, whether by ourselves or others, only represents one aspect of reality.

无论具体内容如何，分类过程再次让我们意识到研究者、表演者和受众所扮演的主动且部分主观的角色。比较性和历史性术语自有其用处，但对文化特异性的探究、对这些特异性与跨文化术语相关联的一系列内涵是否契合的探究、对本土分类体系的探究、以及对围绕现有预期形成和转变的形态的探究，也同样具有价值。即便我们选定了看似最符合研究需求和民族志实际的特定分类，仍可能存在其他线索来划分或整合这些范畴。无论是我们自己还是他人对文类或其他区分的传统命名，都仅代表现实的一个方面。

Finally it is not just outsiders or scholars who disagree about use and interpretation; the creators and performers may do so too and themselves be part of the debate. The target, furthermore, is sometimes a moving one: new genres arise, old ones are developed, manipulated, or reinterpreted, and, whether explicitly or imperceptibly, new practices become established under old names. Contrary to what used to be assumed, such changes and continuities are nothing new but a predictable part of human culture.

最后，不仅外部观察者或学者会在用法和解读上存在分歧；创作者和表演者也可能如此，且他们自身也是争议的一部分。此外，研究对象有时是动态变化的：新的文类不断涌现，旧的文类被发展、操控或重新解读，新的实践无论以显性还是隐性的方式，在旧的名称下逐渐确立。与过去的假设相反，此类变化与连续性并非新鲜事物，而是人类文化中可预见的一部分。
