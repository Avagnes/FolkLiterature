#### 7.3 DIFFERENTIATING AND STUDYING GENRES
#### 区分与研究文类

Whether consciously or not, most researchers get involved at some point in differentiating the kinds of spoken or written conventions that can be broadly summed up in the notion of genre; or, at the least, in using genre or genre-like words to convey their findings or interpret those of others. It is useful to be aware of the issues here, and of the range of genre-terms commonly used or argued over.

无论是否有意识地为之，大多数研究者在某个阶段都会参与区分可被广义概括为 “文类” 的口头或书面惯例；或至少会使用文类或类文类术语来传达自己的研究发现或解读他人的研究成果。了解此处涉及的问题以及常用或存在争议的文类术语范围，是很有必要的。

It is always worth trying to investigate local conventions and taxonomies. This obvious point needs stating in view of the older idea, reflected in Greenway’s still-read Literature among the Primitives (1964:35), that in oral contexts a ‘literary taxonomy’ is the exception, and that any implicit classifications will need formulation by outside observers in terms of their own cultural canons. It is true that local classifications are not always intellectualised or systematic, nor do they necessarily coincide with outsiders’ divisions: the Limba mboro for example covers not only stories but also riddles or proverbs (Ben-Amos 1976:236, Finnegan 1967) while the-to us-‘obvious’ distinction between myths and legends is seen differently in some other cultures (see 7.4.1 below). But there are now many field-based studies to exemplify the occurrence of complex literary taxonomies (Chimombo 1988, Sherzer 1983, Feld 1990, Finnegan 1970:79 and references there).

尝试探究本土惯例与分类体系总是值得的。这一显而易见的观点之所以需要强调，是因为存在一种陈旧观念 —— 格林韦在其仍被广泛阅读的《原始人的文学》（1964：35）中便有所体现 —— 即口头语境中 “文学分类体系” 是例外情况，任何隐性分类都需要外部观察者根据自身文化准则进行构建。诚然，本土分类并非总是经过理论化或系统化的，也不一定与外部观察者的分类一致：例如，林巴语中的 “mboro” 既涵盖故事，也包括谜语或谚语（本 - 阿莫斯，1976：236；芬尼根，1967）；而在我们看来 “显而易见” 的神话与传说的区分，在其他一些文化中则有不同理解（参见下文 7.4.1 节）。但如今已有许多基于田野调查的研究，例证了复杂文学分类体系的存在（奇蒙博，1988；谢尔泽，1983；费尔德，1990；芬尼根，1970：79 及相关参考文献）。

Taking account of these regularities is essential for a full understanding of the ideas and practices of any local art form, if only to place them in context and elicit their peculiar features. As Roger Abrahams put it

We point to genres because by naming certain patterns of expression we are able to talk about the traditional forms and the conventional contents of artistic representation, as well as the patterns of expectation which both the artist and audience carry into the aesthetic transaction.

(Abrahams 1976:193)

考虑这些规律性对于充分理解任何本土艺术形式的观念与实践至关重要 —— 至少有助于将其置于具体语境中，并揭示其独特特征。正如罗杰・亚伯拉罕斯所言：

“我们关注文类，是因为通过命名特定的表达模式，我们能够讨论艺术表征的传统形式与常规内容，以及艺术家和受众带入审美互动中的预期模式。”

（亚伯拉罕斯，1976：193）

The precise delineation of these genres is seldom straightforward, however. One constant problem is how quickly, if at all, to move to comparative genre-terms. Most existing collections and analyses do use such terms, useful vehicles both for understanding your own findings and communicating them to others. But the ethnographic data must also exert their pull, the more so that comparative terms accepted at any one time do not have any absolute or eternal validity.

然而，对这些文类的精确界定往往并非易事。一个始终存在的问题是，是否以及何时转向使用比较性文类术语。大多数现有集子和分析确实使用了此类术语 —— 它们既是理解自身研究发现的有用工具，也是与他人交流的有效载体。但民族志数据也必须发挥其影响力，尤其是因为任何时期被接受的比较性术语都不具有绝对或永恒的有效性。

A further issue is what criteria to use in differentiating genres-if this is thought desirable-and how to express these differences. This is more of a problem than it may seem, due to the confused basis for many generic classifications. There is often a cluster of heterogeneous, sometimes self-contradictory, criteria behind even apparently established words. 另一问题是，若认为有必要区分文类，应采用何种标准，以及如何表达这些差异。这一问题比表面看起来更为复杂，因为许多文类分类的基础都较为混乱。即便是看似既定的术语，其背后往往也存在一系列异质且有时相互矛盾的标准。有时以下所有或大多数常用标准会同时适用 —— 但有时并非如此，此时你将不得不做出一个主观（最好是有意识的）决策：

1 Stylistic or formal features: including length, form of expression (prose? poetry? a mixture?), prosody, structure, type of linguistic expression (‘everyday’ as against symbolic or obscure language?), ‘unity’ or otherwise, and manner of treatment (as in Lüthi’s concept of ‘abstract style’, 1982). Such criteria have a long history in literary theory, sometimes divided into ‘outer form’ (metre or structure) and ‘inner form’ (tone, attitude or purpose, as in Wellek and Warren 1949:231).

1. ==风格或形式特征==：包括长度、表达形式（散文？诗歌？混合体？）、韵律、结构、语言表达类型（“日常” 语言与象征性或晦涩语言？）、是否 “统一” 以及处理方式（如吕蒂的 “抽象风格” 概念，1982）。此类标准在文学理论中有着悠久历史，有时被划分为 “外在形式”（格律或结构）与 “内在形式”（语气、态度或目的，参见韦勒克与沃伦，1949：231）。
==内部形式and外部形式==
2 Subject matter: another commonly used indicator. Thus ‘heroic poetry’ is sometimes defined as ‘about heroes’, and ‘myths’ differentiated from ‘legends’ because about gods or origins. Narratives are often similarly subdivided, as in the many collections separated into ‘animal tales’, ‘wonder tales’, ‘stories about people’, ‘trickster tales’, ‘stories about supernatural beings’, etc. (e.g., Nicoloff 1979, Finnegan 1967), or classified in terms of plot or theme (see 8.2)- convenient divisions but ones that may or may not coincide with stylistic differences. Such basic human subjects as, say, love, mourning, or the origins of the world also lend themselves to corresponding classes of oral art (useful for anthologisers).

2. ==主题内容==：另一常用指标。例如，“英雄诗歌” 有时被定义为 “关于英雄的诗歌”，“神话” 与 “传说” 的区分则在于神话关乎神祇或起源。叙事作品也常被类似细分，许多集子将其分为 “动物故事”“奇幻故事”“人物故事”“骗子故事”“超自然存在故事” 等（如尼科洛夫，1979；芬尼根，1967），或按情节或主题分类（参见 8.2 节）—— 这些分类虽便捷，但与风格差异可能一致，也可能不一致。爱、哀悼或世界起源等人类基本主题，也适用于对应的口头艺术分类（对选集编纂者有用）。

3 Occasion, role and context: when and why a text is produced. Yoruba ijala poetry, for example, is defined through being performed by and for hunters at their gatherings, and songs sung at funerals, during drinking, or in interchanges between lovers are often classified as laments, drinking songs and love poetry respectively (not necessarily the same as songs about death, drink or love). Similarly with lullabies, children’s play songs, funeral oratory, and so on. This apparently simple feature is not always easy to discover and in practice context or role have often just been assumed without detailed investigation (attribution of function is a complex process, only too easily based on theoretical preconception rather than empirical observation, see 6.3).

3. ==场合、功能与语境==：文本产生的时间与原因。例如，约鲁巴语的 ijala 诗歌被定义为猎人在聚会中为自己及同伴表演的诗歌；葬礼上、饮酒时或恋人间交流时演唱的歌曲，常分别被归类为挽歌、饮酒歌和爱情诗（这与关于死亡、饮酒或爱情的歌曲并不一定相同）。摇篮曲、儿童游戏歌、葬礼演说等亦是如此。这一表面上简单的特征并非总能轻易发现，实际上，语境或功能往往未经详细调查便被假定（功能归因是一个复杂过程，极易基于理论预设而非经验观察，参见 6.3 节）。

4 Performance characteristics: sometimes an essential element in stylistic conventions rather than an optional frill (as it might be with written forms); indeed for some genres valued above the purely verbal elements. So how a particular form is performed, in what style, or the role of music or dance become central aspects to investigate (see chapter 5, also Finnegan 1977:121ff.). The precise relation between text and performance can also be relevant, linking with questions about how far particular texts are crystallised or fixed, or how much variation is expected between performances of the ‘same’ work.

4. ==表演特征==：有时是风格惯例的核心要素，而非可有可无的装饰（书面形式中可能如此）；事实上，对于某些文类而言，表演特征比纯粹的口头要素更受重视。因此，某一特定形式的表演方式、风格，或音乐、舞蹈的作用，都成为需要研究的核心方面（参见第 5 章，另见芬尼根，1977：121 页及以后）。文本与表演之间的具体关系也可能具有相关性，这涉及特定文本的固化或固定程度，或 “同一” 作品在不同表演中的预期变异程度等问题。

5 Local terminologies and taxonomies: now often emphasised, whether in the form of special terms or of an aesthetic critical vocabulary. Local terminologies may be more complicated than they seem in many published accounts, however, for the (arguably) crucial terms may not be known to everyone, and may be changing or disputed. Idealised or prescriptive terms, too, do not always correspond to what actually happens on the ground. Comparative words like myth, epic, folktale and so on may themselves play a part in local classifications for it is rare indeed for any culture, wherever it is, to be unaffected by wider scholarly discussion. Indeed it is not uncommon for local intellectuals to play the key role in collecting and shaping the corpus of material with direct feedback on the performers’ interpretations. Some deplore such interactions as ‘artificial’ or ‘fakelore’. Perhaps in some cases this is a fair assessment. But if and when these generalised and perhaps foreign-language terms do play a part in moulding people’s consciousness this is surely merely part of the complex reality the researcher has to comprehend.

5. ==本土术语与分类体系==：如今常被强调，无论是以特殊术语的形式，还是以审美批评词汇的形式。然而，许多出版文献中呈现的本土术语可能比实际情况更为简单 —— 因为（可以说）关键术语可能并非人人知晓，且可能处于变化或争议之中。理想化或规范性术语也并不总是与实际情况一致。神话、史诗、民间故事等比较性术语本身也可能参与本土分类 —— 事实上，任何文化（无论位于何处）都难免受到更广泛的学术讨论的影响，这极为罕见。的确，本土知识分子常常在收集和构建文本 corpus 中发挥关键作用，并对表演者的解读产生直接反馈。一些人谴责此类互动为 “人为的” 或 “伪民俗”。在某些情况下，这或许是公正的评价。但如果这些概括性的、或许是外来的术语确实在塑造人们的认知中发挥作用，那么这无疑只是研究者必须理解的复杂现实的一部分。

6 Specifics of time, place and environment: what initially look like recognisable genres may in practice vary not only between cultures, but also at different times and places within cultures, deployed differently by different individuals or interests. There can be emergent or variable forms whose formulation and enactment can be studied as dynamic historical processes rather than neutral synchronic units. Apparent genres can also change over time as individuals exploit accepted frameworks in developing ways (see Vander 1988:287ff), while older conventions can take on new forms, or new genres be created.

6. ==时间、地点与环境的特异性==：起初看似可识别的文类，实际上不仅可能在不同文化间存在差异，在同一文化内部的不同时间和地点也可能有所不同，不同个体或群体可能以不同方式运用这些文类。可能存在新兴或可变的形式，其构建与践行可被视为动态的历史过程，而非中性的共时单位。随着个体以创新方式利用既定框架，表面上的文类也可能随时间变化（参见范德，1988：287 页及以后），而古老惯例可能呈现新的形式，或新的文类可能被创造出来。
==是否可以找到层级性的关系？亚文类。==

Genre classification can in principle build on any or all of these criteria. In practice several usually overlap-the ‘interweaving of layers’ as Perić-Polonijo puts it (1988:161). But there is also the possibility of conflicting but equally tenable classifications, and different scholars highlight different elements (some influential systems are usefully summarised in Jason 1986:179f, 188f). One account may concentrate on verbal style and form, another on performance or functions, and a category picked out under one of these (the lament, say) might look different under other criteria.

文类分类原则上可基于上述任何一项或所有标准。实际上，多项标准通常相互重叠 —— 正如佩里奇 - 波洛尼约所言，是 “层次的交织”（1988：161）。但也可能存在相互冲突但同样合理的分类，不同学者会强调不同要素（贾森在 1986：179f、188f 中对一些有影响力的体系进行了有益总结）。一种解读可能侧重于口头风格与形式，另一种则侧重于表演或功能，在某一标准下界定的范畴（如挽歌）在其他标准下可能呈现不同面貌。

This once again raises the question of why we engage in genre attribution at all. Given the relative, arbitrary and inconsistent character of this process should we, as Jason puts it, ‘do without “genre”?’ (1986: 187).

这再次引发了一个问题：我们为何要进行文类归属划分？鉴于这一过程具有相对性、主观性和不一致性，正如贾森所言，我们是否应该 “摒弃‘文类’概念”？（1986：187）

That is certainly one possible view. And many scholars are indeed moving away from the earlier preoccupation with genre typologies and the concept of fixed, mutually exclusive and ‘pure’ types. On the other hand, there are recognised conventions and patterns in verbal expression which analysts of verbal art forms rightly wish to study-they are not just imposed by outside scholars. There are also practical reasons why researchers have to face up to decisions about genre classification. These relate to how verbal utterances should be transcribed and represented, specially when-as often-this means presenting oral art forms to a wider or a foreign audience. In compiling an anthology of poetry or a collection of tales, how should they be titled? What principles should be used to group and divide them? Should these be directed to the projected readers expectations? Or base themselves on content or on function? The answers are not self-evident, even if they are sometimes implicitly assumed by apparently neutral (but actually genre-laden) typographical formats or headings.

这无疑是一种可能的观点。事实上，许多学者已逐渐脱离早期对文类类型学以及固定、互斥且 “纯粹” 类型概念的执念。但另一方面，口头表达中存在公认的惯例与模式，口头艺术形式的分析师有充分理由对其进行研究 —— 这些惯例与模式并非仅由外部学者强加。研究者也有实际理由必须面对文类分类的决策问题。这涉及口头表达应如何转录和呈现，尤其是当（情况常常如此）需要向更广泛或外国受众呈现口头艺术形式时。在编纂诗歌选集或故事集时，应如何为作品命名？应采用何种原则进行分组和划分？这些原则应基于预期读者的期望？还是基于内容或功能？答案并非不言而喻，即便有时看似中性（但实际上承载文类内涵）的排版格式或标题会隐含这些答案。

Many thus find that it is still illuminating to work with the concept of genre, if in a more open-ended sense than in the past, perhaps recognising the polythetic rather than single nature of any definitions, perhaps also looking to processes of mobilising conventions and resources rather than to fixed entities. For such purposes the criteria just discussed can still provide pointers to the kinds of regularities and continuities found in many forms of verbal expression.

因此，许多学者认为，即便以比过去更开放的意义使用文类概念（或许承认任何定义都具有多特征性而非单一性，或许更关注惯例与资源的调动过程而非固定实体），文类概念仍然具有启发意义。出于这些目的，上述讨论的标准仍可为识别许多口头表达形式中存在的规律性与连续性提供指引。
