

Classification no doubt enters into all branches of scientific study. It has been particularly prominent, however, in the field of oral arts and traditions. The construction of typologies played a central part in many earlier studies, and in turn linked with the notion of genre, a deeply influential concept within linguistic, philological and literary study, as well as in anthropology, history and folklore. As a result categories such as ‘myth’, ‘folktale’, ‘epic’, or ‘proverb’ take on the air of real and permanent objects in themselves, seemingly the unquestioned basis for scientific taxonomies and the representatives of pure and enduring types. Such classifications, and in particular the concept of genre, have been productive ones, and certainly need to be noted by anyone involved in research on verbal art. As will appear, however, they also involve complexities and controversies of a kind which accord well with recent trends within anthropology to question traditional single-factor classifications and realist definitions.

分类无疑存在于所有科学研究领域中。然而，在口头艺术与传统研究领域，分类尤为突出。类型学构建在许多早期研究中占据核心地位，并进而与 “文类”（genre）概念相关联 —— 这一概念在语言学、文献学、文学研究以及人类学、历史学和民俗学中都具有深远影响。其结果是，“神话”“民间故事”“史诗” 或 “谚语” 等范畴本身仿佛成为真实且永恒的存在，似乎是科学分类体系不容置疑的基础，也是纯粹且持久类型的代表。此类分类（尤其是文类概念）已被证明具有重要价值，任何从事口头艺术研究的人都必须予以关注。但正如后文将要呈现的，这些分类也涉及诸多复杂问题与争议，这与人类学领域近年来质疑传统单因素分类和实在论定义的趋势高度契合。
