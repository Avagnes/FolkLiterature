### 7.5 CROSS-CUTTING THEMES
### 跨文类主题
As well as the accepted-if shifting-boundaries and genres just discussed, certain other themes have been posited. These are not so much the basis for differentiation as strands running through a number of genres and divisions (some have a specifie generic manifestation).

除了上述已被接受（尽管不断变化）的界限和文类外，学者们还提出了其他一些主题。这些主题与其说是区分的基础，不如说是贯穿多个文类和划分的线索（部分主题具有特定的文类表现形式）。

##### 7.5.1 Drama, rhetoric and performance

There is a sense in which all forms of verbal expression-indeed all our actions-can be seen as dramatic performance or, similarly, as rhetoric in the sense of the conventions of argumentation running through so much human communication (see Arnold and Bowers 1984, Perelman and Olbrechts-Tyteca 1969, also references in 5.1 and 7.4.5 above). Such viewpoints challenge imposed boundaries between ‘art’ and ‘life’ or between otherwise separate genres. Should we really draw a line between fully-fledged ‘drama’ or ‘speech-making’ and everyday performances? Are these themes not sometimes as significant as arguably superficial genre-based divisions?

在某种意义上，所有口头表达形式 —— 事实上我们所有的行为 —— 都可被视为戏剧性表演；或者类似地，被视为修辞（rhetoric），即贯穿大量人类交流的论证惯例（参见阿诺德与鲍尔斯，1984；佩雷尔曼与奥尔布赖希茨 - 泰特卡，1969；另见上文 5.1、7.4.5 节的参考文献）。此类观点挑战了人为设定的 “艺术” 与 “生活” 之间的界限，或其他原本相互独立的文类之间的界限。我们真的应该在成熟的 “戏剧” 或 “演讲” 与日常表演之间划清界限吗？这些主题有时难道不比那些可被论证为表面化的、基于文类的划分更重要吗？

##### 7.5.2 Myth and mythic imagination

The term ‘myth’ is used not only as a specifie genre word but also in a more general sense which indicates the somehow symbolising, cosmic, eternity-laden aspects of human consciousness. Myth is thus opposed to ‘history’ (in the sense of secular chronological narrative) or, in its adjectival form, contrasted with such concepts as ‘worldly’, ‘literal’ or ‘temporal’. Sometimes this mythic quality is seen as distinctive to certain forms, but it can equally be presented as permeating, to a greater or lesser extent, many kinds of genres or tellings (for example the ‘mythic mode of consciousness’ in some South American historical narratives, Hill 1988: 8ff).

“神话（myth）” 这一术语不仅用作特定的文类词汇，还被用于更广泛的意义，指代人类意识中某种具有象征性、宇宙性、承载永恒意味的层面。因此，神话与 “历史”（指世俗的编年叙事）相对立；其形容词形式则与 “世俗的（worldly）”“字面的（literal）” 或 “暂时的（temporal）” 等概念形成对比。有时，这种神话特质被视为某些形式所特有，但它同样可被表述为或多或少渗透于多种文类或叙事之中（例如部分南美历史叙事中的 “神话意识模式”，希尔，1988：8 页及以后）。

##### 7.5.3 Narrative

Narrative can be seen not just a specific genre or work, but as a theme running through many forms, non-verbal as well as verbal. This too can be studied in its own right, irrespective of conventional genre classification (see 2.4.8, 8.3.4). Once again this cross-cutting element can emerge in different genres and media, a mark of conjunction among surface differences.

叙事（narrative）不仅可被视为特定的文类或作品，还可被视为贯穿多种形式（包括非口头和口头形式）的主题。无论传统的文类分类如何，叙事本身都可作为独立的研究对象（参见 2.4.8、8.3.4 节）。这一跨文类要素再次体现在不同的文类和媒介中，成为表面差异之下的共性标志。

##### 7.5.4 Other possible schemes

A number of other differentiations also get away from the some-what realist idea of separate genres, or cut across them. Some arise from wider distinctions in particular cultures (as in the example in Fig. 7.2). Others are more general. Among these are the more, or less, useful distinctions between popular and élite forms, or between fixed and fluid genres. More complicated is Roger Abrahams’ account of ‘the complex relations of simple forms’ (Fig. 7.3). As well as considering the ‘structural levels’ of the materials and the dramatic structure (1976:197) he particularly emphasises context in the sense of the level of interaction between performer and audience to draw up four main categories of genres (conversational, play, fictive, and static).

还有其他一些分类方式摆脱了文类相互独立的实在论观念，或跨越了这些观念。部分分类源于特定文化中的更广泛区分（如图 7.2 中的例子），其他则更具普遍性。其中包括通俗形式与精英形式、固定文类与流动文类之间或多或少有用的区分。更为复杂的是罗杰・亚伯拉罕斯关于 “简单形式的复杂关系” 的论述（图 7.3）。他除了考虑材料的 “结构层次” 和戏剧结构（1976：197）外，还特别强调表演者与受众之间的互动层面这一语境，据此划分出四大文类范畴（言语文类、游戏文类、虚构文类和静态文类）。

Bakhtin (1986) has also introduced some interesting differentiations. He first notes the heterogeneity of speech genres (oral and written), including

Short rejoinders of daily dialogue (and these are extremely varied depending on the subject matter, situation, and participants), everyday narration, writing (in all its various forms), the brief standard military command, the elaborate and detailed order, the fairly variegated repertoire of business documents (for the most part standard), and the diverse world of commentary (in the broad sense of the word: social, political)…the diverse forms of scientific statements and all literary genres (from the proverb to the multivolume novel).

(Bakhtin 1986:60–1)

巴赫金（1986）也提出了一些有趣的区分。他首先指出言语文类（口头和书面）的异质性，包括：

“日常对话中的简短回应（这些回应因主题、情境和参与者的不同而极为多样）、日常叙事、写作（所有各种形式）、简短的标准军事命令、详尽细致的指令、种类相当多样的商业文件（大多为标准格式）、以及多样化的评论世界（广义上的：社会的、政治的）…… 科学陈述的各种形式以及所有文学文类（从谚语到多卷本小说）。”

（巴赫金，1986：60-61）

Amidst this variety he distinguishes between primary or simple genres- developed in ‘unmediated speech communion’-and the ‘secondary’ genres of ‘developed and organised cultural communication (primarily written) that is artistic, scientific, sociopolitical and so on’ (1986:62). Primary genres can be absorbed into the secondary, but with altered status, like dialogue appearing in a novel ‘as a literary-artistic event rather than as everyday interaction’ (loc. cit.). These secondary and ‘ideological’ genres are taken to be primarily written, but the distinction can also be applied to ‘everyday’ as opposed to ‘artistic’ genres in an oral context (see also Dorst 1983).

在这种多样性中，他区分了 “初级” 或 “简单” 文类（在 “无中介的言语交流” 中发展而来）与 “次级” 文类（“发达且有组织的文化交流（主要为书面形式），包括艺术、科学、社会政治等领域的交流”，1986：62）。初级文类可被次级文类吸收，但地位会发生变化，例如对话出现在小说中时，“是作为文学艺术事件而非日常互动”（同前引）。这些次级的、“意识形态性的” 文类被认为主要是书面形式，但这一区分也可应用于口头语境中的 “日常” 文类与 “艺术” 文类（另见多斯特，1983）。

These various schemes are not universally accepted, nor will they necessarily fit every cultural situation. However they do draw attention to potentially important contrasts and continuities that might be missed by focusing on only the traditional genres.

这些不同的分类体系并未被普遍接受，也不一定适用于所有文化情境。但它们确实有助于关注那些仅聚焦于传统文类可能会忽略的、具有潜在重要性的对比与连续性。
