### 2.3 口头传统与言语艺术研究的背景
> It is a commonplace that the study of oral traditions and verbal art has moved from earlier preoccupations with origins and the 'old', to more recent emphases on meaning, structure and contemporary dynamics. This is a fair summary as far as it goes, although the one-line development it suggests obscures the many complexities and downplays the intellectual and political contexts of earlier studies. Earlier scholars too interacted with changing historical experiences and philosophies and were at least implicitly concerned with understanding themselves and their contemporary culture through comparative insights into the human condition, not just in a mechanical search for origins.

众所周知，对口头传统与言语艺术的研究，已从早期关注“起源”和“古老事物”的取向，转向了对意义、结构与当代动态的强调。这样的描述虽大体准确，但过于线性化，掩盖了其中的复杂性，也忽视了早期研究的思想与政治背景。早期学者同样与当时不断变化的历史经验与哲学思潮互动，他们至少在潜意识中也试图通过对人类境况的比较性洞察，来理解自身与其当代文化，而非仅仅机械地追溯“起源”。

> The sequence of intellectual fashions is ultimately no different in the study of oral arts and traditions from that in any other sphere of anthropological study. There are however some particular emphases and applications worth noting. As for other fields, one formative strand in earlier studies was the evolutionist model of one-way progress—particularly powerful in the nineteenth century, but still extant in such notions as 'development' and 'modernisation'. This for long directly affected the analysis of anything interpretable as 'early' or belonging to 'oral tradition', particularly 'myths' collected from overseas peoples or 'folklore' from European traditions. The related interest in origins also meant that historical preoccupations—often extremely speculative and generalised—focused interest away from current forms or meanings towards the search for the 'pure', 'original' or 'traditional' stages, 'uncontaminated' by 'outside' influences. Thus stories or songs, provided they could be described as 'traditional', 'folk', or 'tribal', could be analysed as survivals from—and thus 'really' belonging with—some earlier stage of society. The further assumption that oral forms collected from or belonging to the 'old', the colonised or the non-western could be analysed as basically other than those found in European contexts, and as somehow closer to 'nature', also fitted with nineteenth-century (and to some extent still-current) preconceptions: the binary 'us/them' opposition developed in both social theory and popular understanding as one rationale for the modern experience of industrialisation and the expansion of Europe.

在智识流行趋势的更替方面，口头艺术与传统的研究与人类学其他领域并无根本不同。然而，本领域确有一些值得注意的特殊侧重与应用。如同其他学科一样，早期研究中有一条塑成性的思潮是“单向进步”的进化论模式——这一模式在十九世纪尤为强势，至今仍以“发展”“现代化”等概念残存。长期以来，这种思路直接影响了对任何可被解释为“早期”或归入“口头传统”的事物的分析，尤其是来自海外民族的“神话”或欧洲传统的“民俗”。对起源的相关兴趣也使历史性关注（常常极为推测性且概括性）把焦点从当下的形式或意义转移到对“纯粹”“原始”或“传统”阶段的追寻，那些被认为未受“外来”影响的阶段。因此，只要故事或歌曲可被描述为“传统的”“民间的”或“部落的”，它们就可以被分析为某一早期社会阶段的残存物——从而被视为“真正”属于那个早期阶段的文化产物。另一层假设是，从“古老的”、被殖民的或非西方群体收集来的口头形式，在本质上可被视为不同于欧洲语境中的形式，并且在某种程度上更接近“自然”；这一看法契合了十九世纪（并在某种程度上仍存）的成见：即在社会理论和流行认知中形成的“我们/他们”二元对立，成为工业化与欧洲扩张的现代经验的一种合理化论述。

> The influential nationalist movements and the intellectuals associated with them formed another influential strand. This was often closely related to preoccupations with traditions and roots, with a theoretical basis in German romanticism and the concept of the 'folk'. In both nineteenth and early twentieth-century Europe and in more recent ex-colonial nations, the search for national and 'folk' identity has fostered the collection and creation of texts expressing national culture or providing a focus for nation-building and local education—one basis for the often-emotive status of texts classified as 'oral tradition' or 'folklore'. This nationalist theme was central to the development of the discipline of 'folklore' and the many collections and studies associated with it.

有影响力的民族主义运动及其相关知识分子构成了另一条重要脉络。这常与对传统与根源的关注紧密相连，其理论基础可追溯到德国浪漫主义及“民众”（folk）概念。在十九世纪与二十世纪初的欧洲，以及较近的前殖民国家中，对民族与“民间”身份的追寻促进了旨在表达民族文化或作为建国与地方教育焦点的文本的收集与创作——这也是为何被标记为“口头传统”或“民俗”的文本常带有强烈情感色彩的一个根源。民族主义主题对“民俗学”学科的发展及其所产生的大量收藏和研究起了核心作用。

> A further influence came from philology. Research on the development and divergences of languages laid the basis for work drawing on explanations in terms of Indo-European origins and linkages. Philological scholarship also validated the study of texts within a framework of such concepts as a chain of manuscript sources and the analysis of detailed stylistic, grammatical and syntactical patterns—a scholarly tradition based on writing and on the idea of words as the essential constituent units.

语言学（philology）又提供了另一种影响。对语言演变与差异的研究为以“印欧语系起源与联系”为解释框架的工作奠定了基础。文献学/语文学的学术传统也使得在“手稿源流链”及详细文体、语法和句法模式分析的框架下研究文本成为正当：这是一种以书写为基础、将词语视为基本构成单元的学术传统。

> The result of these various influences was extensive collection and publication of texts. Some countries amassed huge archive collections of texts taken down from dictation, written by local scholars, or welded together by collectors or scholars from a mixture of sources.

这些不同影响的综合结果是大量文本的收集与出版。在某些国家，曾形成巨量的档案藏品——这些文本可能是通过口述记录而成、由本地学者所写，或由收藏者/学者将多种来源拼合整理而成。

> Later developments both built on and changed the earlier approaches. Social and political reassessments consequent on experience in and, later, disengagement from empire, and on more recent cultural interactions in a global framework, both reinforced and questioned the binary and evolutionary models. Wider scholarly developments also played a part, among them the general moves in the social sciences (and perhaps in intellectual approaches generally) towards a greater interest in societal conditions, social dynamics, and specific historical processes rather than generalised origins. Also important was the anthropological emphasis on fieldwork and detailed ethnography, turning away from a preoccupation with origins and evolution to understanding contemporary forms in their own setting.

随后的学术发展既承接又改造了早期方法。因帝国经验及其后果（以及随后的脱离）而引发的社会与政治再评估，以及更近的全球文化互动，既强化也质疑了二元化与进化论式的模型。更广泛的学术动向也参与其中，其中包括社会科学（或更大范围内智识取向）朝向更关注社会条件、社会动力学与具体历史过程而非泛化起源的转变。同样重要的，还有人类学对实地调查与详尽民族志的强调，这使研究从对起源与演化的偏执转向在其自身语境中理解当代形式。

> Mastering the details of these historical developments—a subject of study in its own right—may not be essential for contemporary research, but this background does help to put recent views into greater perspective. As always, the theoretical approaches were intertwined with social and political processes as well as with wider intellectual movements, so that now, as earlier, assessing existing or emerging theories demands sensitivity to social and ethical—not just to technical—issues.

掌握这些历史发展细节——本身就是一门学问——对当代研究或许并非绝对必要，但了解这些背景确能把近期观点置于更开阔的视野中。如同以往一样，理论取向与社会政治进程以及更广泛的智识运动交织在一起，因此现在或过去对既有或新兴理论的评估，都需要对社会与伦理问题（而不仅仅是技术性问题）保持敏感。
